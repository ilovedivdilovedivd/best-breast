Breast Augmentation

The size of the implant has the greatest impact on the final appearance; large implants give the greatest upper pole fulness. Stop thinking in cup sizes; no one can guarantee a cup size based on an implant volume.

Although round and shaped implants can achieve a similar result, shaped implants have a more long lasting good result and have the LOWEST complication profile.

Go visit a few ABPS certified/ASAPS member surgeons that specialize in breast surgery.

Position of incisions for breast augmentation.

There are several different incisions that can be used to insert a breast implant. Inframammary fold beneath the breast) periareolar, axillary and umbilical. The umbilical (belly button) route is not commonly used because it does Not improve the results and is only used for saline implants.

The most commonly used incision is in the fold beneath the breast. It is without question the most accurate and versatile incision. All types and sizes of implants can be inserted through this incision and it heals very well, being hidden in a natural skin crease.

The are no disadvantages to this approach. The next most commonly used incision is the periareolar which is around the lower half of the areola fropm the 3 to 9 oclock position.

It too can be used for most types of implants however there is a limit to the size if silicone implant that can be inserted through this incision depeding on the size of the areola.

It is slightly less accurate in that the entire pocket may not be able to be visualized and there is a slightly higher chance of a visible scar or distortion of the breast tissue below the scar.

The axillary incision is also much less often used because in most cases it is a blind procedure where the surgeon does not see inside the pocket and watches the outside of the breast as long instruments are used to make the pocket.

It offers no advantage nor does it improve the results when compared to the two more commonly used incisions and limits the size silicone implant that can be used and because of the axillary hair may have a more visible scar.

All 3 incisions when used carefully and sutured with care will give good results. Your surgeon will choose the incision he or she feels will give you the best resul tand that they are most comfortable using.

Is it worth it to get a breast augmentation to go up only one cup size?

Dr Ali Adibfar Toronto

Increasing the breasts by one cup size is a frequent request and achievable. The best cosmetic result in any particular breast augmentation patient depends on a variety of factors, including: Your individual anatomy, realistic expectations, a thorough discussion with the plastic surgeon about the options, and an understanding of the pros and cons of any particular implant choice.

Proper sizing is not just about the number of cc’s. The thickness of your tissue, breast dimensions which include the width, height, and projection, as well as chest wall width all need to be considered when choosing an implant.

Trying on implant “sizers” of various shapes and volumes while wearing a tight t-shirt, bra, or bathing sit at a preoperative visit will help you and your surgeon choose the optimal implant. There are no manufacturers’ standards for cup sizing in the bra industry.

The cups of a 32 C- D and a 38 C-D are significantly different. Cup size varies from manufacturer to manufacturer and even within styles from any particular manufacturer. There is also no direct correlation between an implant size or shape and resultant cup size.

Keep in mind that following the advice from a surgeon on this or any other website who proposes to tell you exactly what to do without examining you, physically feeling the tissue, assessing your desired outcome, taking a full medical history, and discussing the pros and cons of each operative procedure would not be in your best interest.

I would suggest you find a plastic surgeon certified by the American Board of Plastic Surgery and ideally a member of the American Society for Aesthetic Plastic Surgery (ASAPS) that you trust and are comfortable with.

You should discuss your concerns with that surgeon in person.
