Risks with breast implant surgery in Indiana

Prudent and thoughtful patients always take the time to understand their surgery and the possible risks and complications. I like to think about risks in two groups. In one group are the risks associated with surgery in general.

These include infection, bleeding, risk of blood clot, and possible complications related to anesthesia. Fortunately, in a healthy patient undergoing breast augmentation, the risk of these is low. The second group includes risks/complications/outcomes related to the breast implant surgery itself.
Outcomes that are less desirable include palpable implant edge, rippling, capsular contracture, size too large or too big, loss of nipple senstion, poor scar, etc. In addition, there are outcomes that can lead to further surgery such as deflation or leaking of the implant (is not poisonous), capsular contracture, implant displacement, etc.

Infection involving the implant usually mandates removing the implant for several months followed by replacement. Fortunately, infection involving the implant is very rare. I advise my patients the following to avoid infection-take antibiotic 1 hour prior to any dental procedure and no nipple piercing.

I also advise my patients that they can expect to have another surgery-whether for deflation, to change older silicone implants, etc. at some point after the initial surgery. Implants are like cars-they occassionally require maintenance.
That being said, the overwhelming majority of patients who have implants for the right reasons and choose an appropriate size, are very happy.
