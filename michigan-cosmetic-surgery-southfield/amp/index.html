Mariam Awada, MD, FACS

Name: Mariam Awada, MD, FACS
Statement: Dr. Mariam Awada is the founder of Michigan Cosmetic Surgery, a cosmetic surgery practices in the greater Detroit area. A board-certified plastic surgeon with 23 years experience specializing in Breast Augmentation procedures, she treats each of her patients with individualized attention and compassionate care.

Using advanced technology and minimally invasive techniques, Dr. Awada strives to empower women by making them feel more confident in their bodies and provides them with the knowledge to make informed decisions about which procedures are best for them.

She specializes in breast augmentation, skin tightening, mommy makeovers, body contouring, among other cosmetic surgery procedures. She’s known for her artistic eye, particularly for the anatomy of a woman’s body. She also has expertise in Botox and dermal fillers, including Juvéderm, Restylane, and Belotero.

Dr. Awada graduated from Wayne State University School of Medicine in Detroit, Michigan, where she was inducted into the Alpha Omega Alpha National Medical Honor Society for her outstanding skill and commitment to excellence. She later completed her residency in general and plastic surgery at the prestigious University of Texas Southwestern Medical Center in Dallas, Texas. There, she trained with leading plastic surgeons from around the world, mastering the field’s most tried-and-true techniques while helping to develop groundbreaking procedures.

A RealSelf 500 Top Doctor honoree who has been named one of America’s Top Plastic Surgeons by the Consumers’ Research Council of America, Dr. Awada is a member of the American Society of Plastic Surgery, the American College of Surgeons, the International Society of Aesthetic Plastic Surgery, and the American Society of Aesthetic Plastic Surgery.

Firmness around incision lines under breast is much more firm at 5 weeks. Surgeon says it's scar tissue. Opinions? (photos)
Jan 3, 2020
Scar tissue is a common occurrence after surgery and generally will soften with time.  If you don't notice the scar softening, or if you feel that they seem to be getting thicker or more firm with time I would discuss treatment options with your surgeon as there are things that can be done to soften and minimize the appearance of scar tissue.
