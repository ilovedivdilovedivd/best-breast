Breast Implants with or without a Breast Lift

Before you make a decision on surgery and potential scars, there is no substitute for a consultation and exam with a a board certified plastic surgeon.

Nonetheless, your photos may indicate you would benefit from a breast lift as well as implants.

Unfortunately, breast implants do not do a very good job of lifting the breast when it is droopy. Its possible you would benefit from a shorter scar breast lift such as a round or a lollipop type incision.

Rippling with breast implants

Rippling with all types of breast implants is common. Luckily most of the time, the rippling is not visible and only sometimes palpable. There are many factors that determine how visible or palpable implants will be:

1. Implant type – Saline implants ripple much more frequently and readily than silicone implants because the filler material does not hold its form as well. Also, textured implants tend to ripple more often.

2. Implant location – Implants placed under the muscle tend to have less visible and palpable rippling than implants placed above the muscle.

However, implants placed under the muscle still have the lower 1/3 exposed which can sometimes lead to more noticeable rippling inferiorly.

3. Breast tissue – If more breast tissue is present prior to augmentation, rippling will be less visible and palpable.

Generally, rippling will not be noticeable right away due to swelling after surgery. After a few weeks as the breast settles down, rippling may become more apparent.

Questions about a breast lift

You have too much breast sagging to get a satisfactory result from a breast augmentation only. Your only options would be a breast lift or a combination of the two procedures. If you are satisfied with your breast size then you can reshape your breasts without the use of implants.

One option would be a vertical breast lift with a dermal pedicle autoaugmentation. This procedure uses the breast tissue that you have below the breast crease and uses it to simulate a breast augmentation with a moderate degree of superior fullness.

This will leave you with an incision around your areola and down to the crease. It does leave a scar but in exchange you would have a more youthful breast contour and profile.

Saline implants and rippling

essentially all saline implants ripple regardless of size or fill. the biggest variable determining how visible this will be is the thickness of the tissue around the implant- ie how much skin, fat and breast tissue you have over your implants.

This is the reason almost all plastic surgeons place saline implants under the chest wall muscle. If you are quite thin, I would advice you to consider silicone gel implants .

Why do same size implants look bigger on some women than other women?

Keep in mind that bone structure, skin condition, torso circumference, and many other factors contribute for an implant to look bigger or smaller on woman of the same height and weight. Thats why the plastic surgeon takes a lot of measures for choosing an implant volume for a patient.

Would breast implants automatically give you a breast lift? Or do you necessarily need a lift with implants?

Implants alone will only minimally elevate the position of the nipple. I tell my patients that if they need a lift , they need a lift and that an augmentation alone will not be a substitute.

In most cases when I perform a lift I use an implant to provide fullness in the upper breast and to provide projection and shape. You should have 2 -3 consults with plastic surgeons in your area, look at results and scars and schedule with the doctor you feel most comfortable with.
