Breast Augmentation pricing

Breast augmentation pricing varies widely for a number of reasons. There are wide geographic price differences with prices in Los Angeles and New York City typically being higher than in other areas. Where I practice in Dallas, TX, prices are probably about average compared to most other major urban areas.

However, no matter where you live, it is important to find out exactly what the fee covers. The major determinants of the total fee for the procedure include the surgeon’s fee, the fee for the facility where the procedure is performed, the anesthesia fee and the cost of the implants.

Huntington Beach Implant pricing varies greatly depending on whether you choses saline, round silicone gel or tear dropped shape silicone gel filled implants. It is important to realize that choosing a surgeon who offers a very inexpensive breast augmentation could be a huge mistake.

There are numerous doctors in many cities who are not plastic surgeons that nonetheless perform breast augmentation. Many do the procedure under local anesthesia. The cost of fixing a poorly performed surgery could be much greater than the cost of the initial procedure.

Seek out a plastic surgeon who is board certified by the ABPS, experienced, has excellent reviews and spends time finding out exactly what your goals are. Do not just look for the best deal. Currently, in my practice in Dallas breast augmentation prices vary from about $5200 to $6550. This includes all major fees (surgeon’s fee, facility fee, anesthesia fee and cost of the implants). (Laurence Weider, MD, Dallas Plastic Surgeon)

Breast implant cost augmentation

The cost of breast implant surgery in Huntington Beach may vary significantly. The reasons for the differences are important for any patient considering breast augmentation to understand:

  • implants may be composed of saline, gel or cohesive gel (the ‘gummy bear’ implant). Their prices vary depending on the maker and the quality; saline is generally the least expensive.
  • facility fee and anesthesia options will differ depending on the surgery center and type of anesthesia preferred.
  • surgeon’s charges differ based on experience, training and location

It is important that anyone considering breast implant surgery fully investigate the above factors as well as the training and specific board certification of the surgeon.

Be sure that the surgeon is doing the procedure in a fully accredited facility and not in an office OR that does not have the proper certification. When considering all of the above itemized cost factors, the total amount for breast implant surgery, anesthesia, implant type and facility can range from $7500 – $10,000 for most board certified plastic surgeons. I encourage you to discuss these factors thoroughly with your doctor and staff. (Frank Barone, MD, Toledo Plastic Surgeon)

How Long Do Breast Implants Last?

You do not have to replace your breast implants every 10 years. If your breasts are soft and their shape and size haven’t changed then you do not have to change your breast implants even if they have been in for 14 years.

However, if you are experiencing symptoms as you are describing then you should be examined by a plastic surgeon to make sure that you do not have a leaking or ruptured implant.

If you have a saline implant and the implant leaks, it will deflate and you will notice it immediately. It is not as straight forward with silicone implants and sometimes an MRI is necessary to look for a leak or rupture. (Mohammed Alghoul, MD, Chicago Plastic Surgeon)

How long do breast implants last?

You may have a problem with your left breast implant and probably will need further investigation either through a CT scan/MRI scan, or through surgery. Breast implants do not have a defined lifespan. In theory, all breast implants should last a lifetime.

They are, however, a man-made device and some of them will fail and need to be replaced. Most women who have implants (from the 1980s up to today) have not needed to have them replaced. I would be cautious about someone who is telling you that you must replace the implants every ten years. That simply is not true. (John Zavell, MD, FACS, Toledo Plastic Surgeon)

10 Year Myth

There exists a commonly help misconception among patients that implants need to be replaced every 10 years. The reality is that the failure rate at 10 yrs is approximately 10% (1% per year)If there is no problem with an implant there is no need for any intervention.

While the shells are are made of the same material, device failure in saline and silicone implants manifests in different ways. Saline implants simply deflate and the saline resorbs. Silicone implants typically do not show any signs of failure at the gel (which is inert) typically remains inside the device (see Cohesive Gel).

Current recommendations are for surveillance MRI in order to identify rupture.While your specific concerns are necessarily indicative of device failure, consult with your board certified plastic surgeon. (Donovan Rosas, MD, Nyack Plastic Surgeon)
