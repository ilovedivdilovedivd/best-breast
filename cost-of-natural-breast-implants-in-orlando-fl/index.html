In women with narrow chest diameter, choosing the right implant can be difficult for several reasons. When women have small chest circumferences, an implant with a narrow base dimension may be necessary for a proper fit on the chest wall.

It’s important that you discuss these issues with your surgeon before proceeding so everyone understands your priorities.

Breast implant scars

The three incisions used most commonly are in the breast crease, along the bottom of the areola, and in the armpit. Depending on how you scar, all three of these scars generally are favorable over time.
In most bathing suits, you should not be able to see the bottom of the breast along it’s crease or around the areola. However, a poorly healed or a raised scar in the armpit will be visible if you raise your arm in a tank top or bathing suit.

Breast Augmentation Scar Placement Options

Scars associated with breast implants in Orlando FL are typically placed in the following areas: Under the armpit, on the underside of the breast, and along the areola, which is the preferred method of approach in my practice for a number of reasons.

Implants may also be place through the belly-button, if your surgeon of choice happens to use that approach. All approaches can be done with small incisions, typically 2 cm or less, even with larger implants. The only scars that would be evident in a bathing suit would be those seen if your surgeon uses an armpit approach and possibly the belly button approach.
I recommend asking your surgeon the surgical approach her/she prefers and how long the incisions typically are.

WHY do Breast Implants RIPPLE?

ALL breast implants ripple. The rippling is a function of the action of the filler on the surrounding shell. Saline breast implants tend to ripple more than silicone gel filled breast implants. In addition, textured surface implants will ripple more than smooth surfaced implants.

This basic fact is the major reason why we cannot place just any size implant into any woman and expect an attractive result. For an attractive result, the patients PRE-surgical breast tissue must be able to cover a prospective implant.
Placing a wider implant than the available breast tissue “roof” means that the uncovered areas (including the ripples) will be under the skin and be felt and seen. Since small breasts women, by definition, have less breast tissue to act as a cover, these woman cannot have large implants without having these implants appear “fake” ; too round and full of folds and ripples.

If you do not believe me, read the tens of sorrowful letters by patients who still cannot understand why they look the way they do with large implants. Ask your surgeon to match your breasts with the most suitable round, smooth silicone gel filled implants (less ripples).

Silcone vs. Saline Breast Implant Rippling

I agree with previous posts: in general, silicone breast implants tend to ripple less than saline breast implants, even when the saline breast implants are filled to the maximum recommended volume or slightly over filled.

Additionally, to minimize the appearance of ripples and palpable implant edges, breast implants on thin women should generally be placed under the muscle.

Fixing asymmetric breasts

Your problem is not that uncommon. Perfect symmetry is not possible with breasts. No woman has perfectly symmetric breasts. When there is a big difference (as your 200cc difference) you canexpect a lot better symmetry after surgery.

As for the long term effects, your breasts will continue to age. Gravity still works on breasts with implants. From my experience, your larger breast will still be slightly larger, but much closer in size after surgery.

Both breasts will age and sag in time. it is possible that the (now) larger one will sag more. Still, you will enjoy the results for a very long time.

Orlando Breast implant ruptures

Saline implant ruptures can be detected relatively quickly as the saline generally flows quickly out of the shell. You’ll probably notice deflation within a day or so. Cohesive silicone gel implants take longer to notice if they have ruptured as they stay relatively intact for a longer period of time.

You may feel firmness or notice the affected breast look misshapen. For better confirmation of rupture you will need to get an MRI performed.
