Many years but probably not forever

Gainesville Breast implants can last years or even decades, if no issues, such as capsular contracture, implant rupture, or implant migration occur. However, they are not considered “lifetime devices.” The FDA recommends MRI screening 3 years after silicone implants are placed, and every 2 years thereafter.

Many plastic surgeons advise their patients to consider swapping out their breast implants after 10-15 years. If you have a rash, pain, and the perception of air pockets, you should see your plastic surgeon for an evaluation. (Lara Devgan, MD, MPH, New York Plastic Surgeon)

How long to breast implants last?

The manufactures of breast implants suggest in their literature that implants should be replaced every 10 -15 years.

However, I have had patients that have had them in for many years past that timeframe. Many of the newer devices have thicker shells, better functioning valves and different viscosity for gel filling.

Although your implants may seem to be okay on the outside, there could be an issue that you are unaware of. Making sure that you have regular diagnostic tests such as Mammograms, Breast Ultrasounds or Breast MRI’s can help to determine how well your older implants are holding up.

There are also some signs that there may be an issue that you can look for such as “hardening” of the breast. If the shell has degraded and some of the silicone is leaking or bleeding out, your body may increase the thickness of the scar tissue or capsule surrounding the implant making your breast feel firm or harder than before.

This could be a sign that it’s time to make a change. (Suzanne M. Quardt, MD, Palm Springs Plastic Surgeon)

Breast Implants Should be Replaced Every 10 Years

Typically, breast implants should be replaced about every ten years. Not doing so raises your risk of rupture and other complications. (Anna I. Wooten, MD, FACS, Pittsburgh Plastic Surgeon)

Lifespan of Breast Implants

There is no absolute time at which breast implants need to be replaced. If you have saline implants they can stay in place as long as they are intact. If they leak you would see a loss of volume on the affected side. The FDA recommends that three years after placement of a silicone filled breast implant a patient undergo an MRI of her breast and every two years thereafter to look for a silent rupture.

A ruptured implant should be removed and replaced. It has been common practice among plastic surgeons to replace older implants in an effort to avoid a rupture. Ten years is an arbitrary number.

The rash and tenderness that you are describing warrants a visit to your surgeon for a diagnosis. (David K. Funt, MD, Long Island Plastic Surgeon)

When do implants need to be exchanged

Breast implants usually do not need to be removed even after an extended period of time. However, there is a small percentage of implant failure, on the order of 15% after 15 years. If you have developed a significant change in the shape of your breasts, discomfort, redness, or new lumps you should contact your surgeon for a thorough evaluation.

This exam may include an MRI exam if you have silicone implants to rule out rupture.

I routinely have my breast implant patients return for an annual exam to catch problems early.

If there is a problem most implants can be removed and replaced with an outpatient surgical procedure. (Andrew Goldberg, MD, Fairfax Plastic Surgeon)

Choosing Implants that Don’t Look “Top-Heavy”

Fortunately there are many different styles, shapes and sizes of implants that a patient can choose to create whatever look is desired.

There are low profile and moderate profile shaped implants that provide less projection and in turn a more natural look. Also, going with a size (or fill) that fits your body and breast measurements will help to prevent the “top-heavy” look.

When selecting a plastic surgeon, you can ask if they do “implant sizing” where they allow you to try on the implants during your consultation or pre-operative appointment in a sports bra to get a general idea of how you will look. Also, the best advice is to not listen to well meaning friends or Google as often you can receive misinformation from these sources. Your best bet is to talk to your board certified plastic surgeon about your concerns. (Suzanne M. Quardt, MD, Palm Springs Plastic Surgeon)

You don’t want to look like a stripper?

Though ultimately the size of a woman’s breasts are based on her desires and sense of aesthetics, the surgeon is there to provide educated guidance. A rule of thumb is that the base width of the breast implant should be equivalent to the width of the patient’s chest wall (from sternal border to armpit).

If a patient is very petite, large implants will appear unnatural. If a patient is thickly built, then large implants will make her look more stocky and matronly. In addition, large heavy implants will compress the patient’s naturalbreast tissue and stretch her skin envelope more than a more moderate size choice.

With these concepts in mind, a woman can begin a frank discussion with her surgeon. (David K. Funt, MD, Long Island Plastic Surgeon)
