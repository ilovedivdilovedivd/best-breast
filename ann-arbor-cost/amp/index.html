How long do breast implants last in Ann Arbor?

That’s really a difficult question to answer because there are many factors that will affect the longevity of a breast implant.

In your case, you may be experiencing some problems with your implant that need to be addressed such as the cause of the rash and “air pockets”.

If you had your implants done 14 years ago they are most likely saline implants and the “pockets” may represent riplling or a possible deflation.

You should see a plastic surgeon and have your situation evaluated including having mammagrams, ultrasound and possibly an MRI.

If the implant is leaking or if the implant shell has disrupted the implant should come out and be exchanged.

Generally, saline implants last 10 years or more but can leak under some circumstances. The newer silicone gel implants with cohesive gel are more durable and less likely to leak.

The most important advice is to have an evaluation and discuss your options. (Joel B. Singer, MD, New York Plastic Surgeon)

Replacement of Breast Implants and How long Breast Implants Last

When to replace breast implants is a very complex topic. There are a number of reasons to replace implants. They include:

  1. Rupture
  2. Capsular Contracture
  3. Rippling
  4. Size Change
  5. Changing between silicone and saline

There is no recommended interval at which you must change your implants. We always advise our patients that breast implants are not lifetime devices and therefore you should expect to replace them at least once in your life.

For Mentor Saline implants in Ann Arbor the rupture rate is 1.8% per year and for Mentor Silicone implants the rupture rate is 1% at 6 years. If a rupture is found then replacement of both implants in indicated.

Please note: both Allergan and Mentor have a warranty replacement program in the event of rupture. In our practice in Richmond, Virginia we have seen patients with both saline and silicone who have gone for over 20 years without issues. If you believe you are experiencing a rupture then see your plastic surgeon as soon as possible. As for the redness and air pockets you are experiencing, there could be a number of causes.

You should schedule an appointment with your plastic surgeon as soon as possible for a complete examination. (Neil J. Zemmel, MD, FACS, Midlothian Plastic Surgeon)

How to choose breast implant size

Breast implants come in many different sizes and more importantly different shapes. A certified experienced plastic surgeon will suggest the best size and shape for your unique anatomy and desires.

There are implant sizers to help you make your decision. I usually suggest my patients take a section of panty hose and fill it with rice to make a soft filler for your bra.

A 300cc implant would be equal to 10 oz of rice. This is not a perfect fit but will give you an idea. Share your concerns and desires with your surgeon and your new contour will be just perfect. (Andrew Goldberg, MD, Fairfax Plastic Surgeon)

Best is to pick implant sizes based on your breast and chest width and not the volume. When the width of the implant fits the width of your chest, it will look nice and fit your frame.

If they are too small, you will have agap between them. If they are too wide, they will make you look top heavy. (Lawrence Scott Ennis, MD, FACS, Pensacola Plastic Surgeon)

Breast implant sizes chart

Choosing a size for a breast augmentation in Ann Arbor can be confusing. More important than the size of the implants is what you want to look like after the surgery.

Sometimes patients have a volume in mind that doesn’t match the look they want to achieve. In theory,the best way to help with determining your implant size would be if you could “see” yourself with implants in Ann Arbor. Although computer simulations and 3-D are useful, they are only pictures.

I have found that the breast implant external sizing system (by Mentor) is a more personal method for patients. It allows you to place specially designed implant shapes over your breasts. (This is not the same as simply putting an implant in your bra.)

The sizing system gives a fairly realistic idea of how your breasts will look with a certain size implant. Once a shape you like is determined, then your surgeon can narrow down the implants to 2-3 sizes and you and your surgeon can finalize the choice. (John Zavell, MD, FACS, Toledo Plastic Surgeon)

Finding the right breast implant size

Finding the correct implant size can be so stressful for my patients. There are so many different opinions offered by my colleagues; that doesn’t make it any easier. There are two trains of thought:

  1. Use anatomical measurements to determine the absolute best or proportional implant size for the patient
  2. Give the patient what she wants

Arguments could be made to support both ways of thinking. I think some patients don’t have a lot of confidence or don’t know exactly what they want. They respond well to doctors with confident opinions about what is the “right implant” for them. Doctors that follow train of thought 1 are best suited for these patients. I think some patients are very sure of what they want cannot tolerate doctors dictating what size is best for them.

They will either be talked into the “best implant” and be dissatisfied with the result or find the doctor that will comply with their wishes. Either way, the best advice for a patient is to consult more than one PS. I think most patients are surprised how similar the final implant selection is for each doctor. (Bradley A. Hubbard, MD, Dallas Plastic Surgeon)
