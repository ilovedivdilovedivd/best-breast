A breast augmentation procedure in Memphis has several costs involved. Typically this procedure is performed in an operating room and requires anesthesia, which have a cost associated with those services. The implants and surgeon’s fees are another cost.

Here in central Texas, the total price of this procedure varies from $4,600 to $7,000 depending on a number of factors. It is most important that you choose a ABPS Board Certified Plastic Surgeon and feel that you communicate well with this individual.

Communication with the surgeon and staff is extremely important. Also, having realistic expectations and being able to communicate this effectively will help to ensure a positive outcome. (Rocco C. Piazza, MD, Austin Plastic Surgeon)

Breast augmentation prices vary by surgeon and location

Like most services, breast augmentation prices vary by surgeon and location. The total price can range anywhere from $4,500 to $14,000. Be sure when you are comparing costs that you understand the TOTAL cost, which means: surgeon’s fee, implant cost, facility fee and anesthesia fee.

One factor is whether you choose saline or silicone gel implants (silicone implants are more expensive). Please don’t fly to South America for “bargain” prices to pay less than the costs listed above. I strongly encourage you to find a trustworthy and reputable board certified plastic surgeon who is located within a reasonable driving distance from your home.

Saving a few hundred dollars will not be very satisfying if you need to meet with your plastic surgeon and it is inconvenient to get to the office. (James N. Romanelli, MD, FACS, Long Island Plastic Surgeon)

Memphis Breast Implant Pricing Depends on Type of Implants and Where You Live

When a breast augmentation is performed, there are usually three separate components to the price. The first component is the surgeon’s fee, the second is the facility fee, which pays for the use of the operating room, and the third is the purchase of the implants.

The fees for these components vary across the country. Silicone implants are more costly than saline implants. Therefore, a breast augmentation utilizing silicone implants would be more expensive than an augmentation performed with saline implants. All of these fees vary across the country. The American Society for Aesthetic Plastic Surgery indicates that the average surgeon fee for a silicone breast augmentation is $3,694.

The average surgeon’s fee for an operation utilizing saline implants is $3,308. This price does not include the purchase of the implants nor does it include the anesthesia or the facility fee. Package prices for a breast augmentation may vary from $3,800 up to $12,000. It’s very important when considering any type of plastic surgery to know the background and credentials of the surgeon. It is also very important to know the accreditation of the surgical facility in which the procedure is going to be performed.

How long do implants last? What is the duration? How often to Replace?

In general 10 years is a good rule of thumb for both saline and gel implants. However, if at 10 years you are happy with the look and feel of your saline implants then there is no need to do anything.

With Gel implants it is important to check the integrity of the implant, because a breakage of the implant is “silent” and can lead to an increase in capsular contracture. Implant replacement is often much easier than the initial surgery, my patients often go back to work the next day and take little to no pain medication. (Brian K. Reedy, MD, Columbus Plastic Surgeon)

How long do implant last?

Implants are not meant to be lifetime devices. If a woman has had her implants for 20 years and they are not causing her a problem, there is no reason to take them out simply due to a period of time. It’s important to remember that surgery is not without risks.

It would be unfortunate if one were to have their implants removed simply because they are greater than 10 years old and developed a complication due to the re-operation. It’s recommended that if gel implants are placed, a MRI is done three years afterwards and then every two years after that to detect a “silent” rupture. (Quintessa Miller, MD, FACS, Las Vegas Plastic Surgeon)

Choosing the Size of Brast Implants

Most modern plastic surgeons in Memphis use a “dimensional” approach when planning breast augmentation. The approach uses over a dozen measurements of the breast, chest wall, and tissue thicknesses in order plan your operation. This approach also leads to a range of sizes that will give you a balanced look. Within the size range you can pick a more conservative or a more liberally sized implnats according to your desires. We then try on implants in a form fitting tank top to give our patients an approximate picture of what to expect. In our practice we strive to give our patients the most balanced, natural look. (Neil J. Zemmel, MD, FACS, Midlothian Plastic Surgeon)

Choosing the ideal breast implant size

When choosing your ideal breast implant size, your decision should take into account two things: what looks good to you when you try sample implants on, and what your doctor recommends. When choosing an implant, it may be too large if the width of the implant is such that it doesn’t ‘fit’ your chest. People with narrower chests can handle smaller implants than people with wider chests.

IF the implant is too wide it will cause the two implants to touch in the middle (synmastia or ‘uniboob’) or will stick out under the arms. You should talk to your surgeon who has extensive experience with breast implants and who is properly certified. Board Certification in Plastic Surgery is what you are looking for. American Board of Plastic Surgery or the Royal College of Physicians and Surgeons of Canada certification in Plastic Surgery are the only certifications you can trust. (Martin Jugenburg, MD, Toronto Plastic Surgeon)
