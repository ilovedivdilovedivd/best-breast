Periareolar Incisions

A periareolar approach is still used by many surgeons today. As a function of length/size, it can be somewhat limiting relative the inframammary fold approach. The incision was more popular in the era of the saline implant (during the moratorium on the use of silicone implants).

Saline implants which are placed empty and filled in-vivo did not neccessitate large access incisions. The limited nature of these incisions has made them slightly less popular with the dawn of the cohesive gels which are stiffer and require larger incisions.

A periareolar approach may or may not be suitable depending upon the unique anatomy of the patients as well as their implant goals. Generally, patients will need an areolar diameter of at least 3.5 cm (the average is approximately 4 cm).

The incision is placed over the lower curvature of the areola and will usually need to be 3-5 cm (depending upon the size and type of implant desired). The use of a Keller funnel can sometimes facilitate the use of a larger implant with a smaller access incision.

With regards to your specific question, it would be difficult to make a valid recommendation without an in person exam and measurements. Depending on the measurements and your desire for a moderate sized gel you may or may not be a good candidate.

Another point of consideration is the (slightly) increased risk of capsular contracture in the setting of a peri-areolar approach attributed to biofilm and the need to dissect through the breast glandular tissue. As always, discuss your concerns with a board certified plastic surgeon (ABPS).
Do I need a breast lift?

I think your best result would be achieved with a submuscular, silicone implant and a doughnut mastopexy. I believe without a mastopexy, your results would be disappointing. Based on your pictures and the size of your areolas, I believe the doughnut mastopexy is your best choice.

This type of mastopexy will lift the breasts and reduce the areola size. The scars usually fade well with time. Based on your chest width, I would suggest a high-profile implant. This will allow for projection and fullness.

Breast-feeding is possible after a doughnut mastopexy, though not 100% guaranteed. Though unlikely, there is some risk with loss of ability to breast-feed. If you really want to be 100% able to breast-feed, do not have the breast augmentation nor mastopexy.
This may not be a bad choice either because the breast will certainly change after breast-feeding.

Breast implants through tummy tuck incision

Is it possible to place breast implants though a tummy-tuck incision and avoid additional scars?

The answer is yes, it is possible to do it safely and accurately. However, the key is that your surgeon has to be comfortable doing so.

Not all surgeons are trained or familiar with this technique and if so, you are better off having it done through the traditional incisions. The important thing is that whoever you finally choose to do your surgery should be certified by the American Board of Plastic Surgery.

Breast implants with fibrocystic breasts

Fibrocystic disease is a common condition within the breast and can develop in many women as they age. The cysts and lumps are not breast cancer or even precancers, though they do make the breast more difficult to examine and cause frequent mammograms and ultrasounds and sometimes breast biopsies.

We feel that unless specific changes are being followed on your mammogram, you should be able to have a sub muscular silicone gel breast implant. The implant will push the breast tissue up and will not prevent a good breast self examination.

Length of Time Breast Implants Last?

This question is asked frequently by patients. If you have implants regardless of age and they feel natural and have no associated problems such as pain or capsular contractures then I would leave them alone.

If however you are experiencing pain or hardness or a study reveals some leakage then I would consider an implant exchange with removal of the capsule.

Do you need a breast lift?

It looks like you will need a breast lift – I do not think you will have a nice result with just breast implants. The breast lift will improve the placement of the nipple/areola complex on the breast mound and give you a “perkier” look.
I would want to examine you in person and see what your goals are before discussing doing the breast augmentation / lift procedure in 1 stage or two. If you were concerned about the size of your areola, the breast lift will also help with that.

Silicone implants

For the most natural feel and look, I would recommend silicone implants placed using the dual-plane method. However, this can only be confirmed by being seen in person by a board certified plastic surgeon.
