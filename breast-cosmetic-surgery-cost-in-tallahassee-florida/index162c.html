Tallahassee Breast Implant Longevity

Breast implants are long-lasting but are not meant to “last a lifetime”. Implants may last for one year or may last for several decades. It is thought that the newer style silicone gel implants will likely last longer because they are made differently.

The outer shell is much more durable and the inner material is now a cohesive gel that is less likely to rupture and cause spillage like the first and second generations.

The implants currently being produced are of the fourth and fifth generation and have gone through several modifications over the years.

If you are experiencing changes in your breasts, then you should speak with your plastic surgeon regarding further investigative studies such as an MRI or an ultra sound.

If these studies indicate a possible rupture or disruption of the integrity of the implant then consideration for replacement should be made. (Michael D. Cohen, MD, Baltimore Plastic Surgeon)

There is No Single Number to Indicate How Long Breast Implants Will Last

There is no single number to indicate how long breast implants will last. There are many types of breast implants and consequently, there are different leakage rates for each one. All implants have an outer Silastic shell. It is within this shell that the contents are contained.

Some implants are saline filled and some are filled with silicone gel. The outer shell can be smooth or it can be textured. A textured surface is more of a roughened surface. In general, it is thought that the implants with the roughened surface have a slightly higher leakage rate than those with a smooth surface. It is commonly accepted that the most frequent cause of leakage is fold flaw. Fold flaw means a weakening along the edge of a natural fold of the implant. All implants are underfilled. This means that there is room for the contents to move back and forth, simulating a more natural breast-like feel.

Because of the natural folds that occur because of this, there is potential for weakening along these folds and subsequent leakage. If a saline implant leaks there is a rather rapid change in the size of the breasts. It becomes quite obvious in a short period of time that the saline has leaked out of the implant. A leak in a silicone implant is more difficult to determine. A person can have a small leak and have it not be noticed. To diagnose a silicone leak it is often necessary to perform an MRI. Consequently, there could be leakage rates associated with the silicone implants that are undiagnosed. Saline implants are filled by the surgeon at the time of surgery. If these implants are slightly overfilled by 10-15% one would expect that the leakage rate would be less. This would be due to the fact that there would be less inclination toward the fold flaw. Commonly quoted figures regarding implant leakage are 1% per year. That would indicate that in a ten-year period of time one out of ten breast implants would fail. In general, it is probably reasonable to assume that in the first ten years they probably will not leak. In the second ten years they might leak and in the third ten years they probably will leak. (John J. Edney, MD, Omaha Plastic Surgeon)

How to choose correct implant size

I am glad you asked this question because it is the key to whether or not you will be happy with your result. First, if the implant size and dimensions are in proportion to your frame, it will look natural and not like a stripper.

For any particular body frame, there are several implant styles, with varying sizes and proportions, that will fit that frame. So, you will have several different choices that will fit you, some will be slightly larger and some slightly smaller. This is where communication between you and your surgeon becomes critical.

The visual look that you want needs to be communicated to your surgeon. My office asks all our patients to bring in pictures of breast sizes and proprotions that both appeal and don’t appeal to the patient.

This helps me understand your aesthetic sense and is a valuable means of communication. In the office, after taking your measurements to find the implants that will fit your frame, we then try on different implants to see which ones you like.

We look at them in a bra and with different types of tops on to make sure they look good in the clothes your wear. We usually do these “sizing” visits 2 or 3 times, making sure you select the same implant each time.

By going to this effort prior to surgery insures that both the patient and I are very happy with the results. I also remind my patients and friends that the only people we know have breast implants are the ones who are “advertising” it. There are lots of women with breast implants and you would never guess it. (Tracy Pfeifer, MD, Manhattan Plastic Surgeon)

Breast implant size

Breast implant size in Tallahassee is determined by both your plastic surgeon (board certified) and you the patient. The implant should conform to your particular anatomy and body type. The implant can only be as large as your tissues/skin will accomodate. Often times patients want large implants that don’t necessarily correspond to their body type or are too large for the skin envelope or tissues.

There are certain measurements your surgeon will take such as breast width, which help guide the implant selection process. Once you have agreed upon a range of implant sizes based on your anatomy, the surgeon should use a sizer or a temporary implant that is inserted during the actual surgery to assess the elasticity of your tissues. This sizer helps to guide the ultimate size selection and helps to accurately shape and develop the pocket where the implant will reside. Please consult with one or more local plastic surgeons to discuss which size is best for you.

You may also try on some of the different size implants before hand in the office to get a rough idea of how the implants will look when completed. (William Bruno, MD, Beverly Hills Plastic Surgeon)

Choosing the right size implant

I feel that the patient needs to be educated to make the best decision possible when choosing implant size in Tallahassee. I encourage my patients to bring in a picture, or pick out a picture from our photo album, of what they are looking for.

I will measure their breast width to determine what profile best suits their frame, and will go over the differences in the profiles of the implants as far as diameter and projection to educate her. We will then “try on” a series of implant samples in a bra to determine what size she is most happy with. This gives me a starting point to know what volume, projection and diameter she is looking for and what will suit her frame. Ultimately, the choice is hers. (Amy T.
