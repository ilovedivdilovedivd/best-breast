Breast Implant replacement through the axilla

Can I get away with implants without a lift?

I am asked this question on almost a daily basis in my practice and I’ve also seen a number of patients who have tried to “avoid a lift” and another surgeon has convinced them that this was possible with large implants.

Unfortunately, when your nipple has drifted below the level of the inframammary fold, a lift is unavoidable for an aesthetically pleasing shape. Without a lift and just implants alone, your breasts will look matronly–overly large and droopy.
I can’t tell you how many women I’ve seen who are extremely dissatisfied with the results of an augmentation alone when their original surgeon should have done a lift at the same time. The good news is that your inframammary fold is still relatively high which will allow for a nice degree of lift.

Scars do take up to 18 months to fully mature but they do improve over time. The vertical breast scar is the one that patients are the most apprehensive about, but it is the scar that heals the best.

It is important to consult with at least 2 board certified plastic surgeons (not cosmetic surgeons) to discuss your options in more detail. An in-person exam is the only way to verify which procedure(s) are best suited to you.

Implant placement

There are two general categories as to how breast implants arte placed:

1 Above the pectoralis muscle
2 Below the pectoralis muscle
The second alternative (below the muscle) has some variations as to wether other muscles are used to cover the lower and side parts of the implant.

Above the muscle looks great and is less painful but has a higher rate of capsular contracture (tightening around the breast). Under the muscle is more painful (which can be minimized by use of a pain pump), but has a softer look in the upper part of the implant.

Moderate plus vs high profile

The profile is based on your chest width and the volume you chose. If you chose a big volume but have a narrow chest, a high profile implant will be used so that you don’t have an excess of breast on the side.

As you go smaller in volume, the profile (projection) gets less so then you get into the moderate profile range.

Signs of capsular contracture following breast augmentation
