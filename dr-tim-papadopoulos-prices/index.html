Implant size and breast size varies depending on several factors

This is a very hard question to answer, and that is the reason that we have personal consultation before surgery. Implant size and breast size varies based on your chest width, tissue elasticity, and your desires.

If you are not comfortable with the size that your surgeon is offering you, discuss this with him and also get a second consultation.

Breast Augmentation and Breast Feeding

The ABILITY to breast feed, all other factors being equal, MAY be hampered with Breast Augmentation depending on the number of breast ducts and glandular units interrupted by the operation. This does NOT mean that a Breast Augmentation automatically would prevent you from being able to breast feed – just that it may not happen or not be as efficient.

If you are able to breast feed, you will NOT hurt your baby by doing so. Multiple studies failed to demonstrate any adverse effects of breast feeding by women who had breast augmentation.

Can you get away with 225cc implants?

They show asymmetric (size and shape) breasts with some constriction and ptosis (droop). Implants do not “lift” breasts — they simply fill the skin brassiere. So adding 225cc (relatively small for your height) implants is certainly possible, but you will likely have a “rock in the sock,” “Snoopy dog,” or waterfall appearance.

None of these is particularly attractive and nothing like your goal photo, which shows a mastopexy (breast lift), which can create a higher, perkier, nicely-shaped result. That being said, your nipples do not appear to be below the level of your inframammary creases, so with enough implant volume to fill your loose breast skin, you do not “need” a lift.
But if you don’t want to be too big, then shaping incisions (breast lift of one of several types) will need to accompany your smaller implant choices to give you something close to your goal photo.

But only if you choose an ABPS-certified, experienced plastic surgeon who agrees to perform what is going to give you the “best” outcome, not the “get by” outcome that ultimately will cause dissatisfaction and probable re-operation.

Your choice. Implants alone can yield a good outcome, but only if you choose implants “large enough” to satisfactorily “fill” your skin brassiere. Larger will help to accomplish this, but if you don’t want larger, then you have to accept the cost and scars of a skin-tightening breast lift and the implant size you request.
A properly-performed breast lift will require the removal of about one ounce of skin, so you must understand that the difference between 225cc and 300cc is only 2 1/2 ounces (or less than a quarter of a can of soda pop).

Safe to fly with breast implants

Implants are not, as far as I know, affected by altitude or high pressure. I understand your concerns in regards to limitations and rules to follow after having breast surgery. There are no limitations when having breast implants.

I have many patients flying in for surgery for breast augmentation and they are able to fly home within a few days. During the recovery period, it is important to avoid any heavy-lifting and strenuous activity for the first few weeks after surgery to avoid implant malposition and/or discomfort.
