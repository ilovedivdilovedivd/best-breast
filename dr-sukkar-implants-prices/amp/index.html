Breast Feeding with Breast Implants

Generally breast implants do not negatively affect a woman’s ability to breast feed.

This is due to the fact that the implant does not disrupt the glands and ducts necessary to breast feed, especially when implants are placed in the sub-pectoral region (under the muscle).

Too old for breast augmentation.

But it is never too late to start feeling better about your body. Be sure to see a board certified plastic surgeon to get the right plan for your body.

30 years old, 1 child, getting silicone implants. Do I need a lift or can I get away with just big implants?

Unfortunately the decision to pursue large implants will only lead to disappointing results and I would strongly recommend a breast lift with much more appropriately sized implants.

This will not only improve your breast shape, volume, and symmetcy, but your results will also last for a longer period of time.

Implants Above or Below

The bottom line is this: would you mind if you could see rippling or the outline of the implant in the upper and inner breast region, i.e. the ‘fake look’? If you wouldn’t, then by all means get them placed in the subglandular position.

If you don’t want a ‘fake look’, but instead want a softer look in the upper pole region, with the least risk of rippling and capsular contracture, then you should consider subpectoral placement. However, subpectoral placement will cause a decrease in strength to your pectoralis muscles that may last for a few months to a year or more.

Recovery Following Breast Augmentation

All aspects of your breast augmentation, including the recovery time, are influenced, in large part, by your selection of a surgeon.

If you are considering a breast augmentation it is very important that you select a surgeon who is properly trained and highly experienced in the procedure of breast augmentation.

Your surgeon will have developed, over time, a very effective way not only to perform the augmentation but to manage your postoperative recovery. The best way for you to reduce your recovery time is to follow the directions of your surgeon.

You will be given direction regarding the movement of your arms and shoulders, the position that you sleep in and the type of bra that you wear afterward. If you follow these directions then your recovery time should not be prolonged.
The pain or discomfort that could follow a breast augmentation is normally controlled well by the pain medicines prescribed by your surgeon. If you follow the directions of your surgeon you should probably expect to be recovered within three to four weeks after your procedure.

Saline Breast Implants and Improving Technology: Is there anything new out there for breast implants

In the United States, saline breast implants are still very popular and are often the first choice for women who are seeking primary breast augmentation. Silicone is not always the best first option for women with normal amounts of breast tissue.
Saline implants continue to improve in terms of their design and risk of complications including capsular contracture and leakage. There are some newer studies in Europe that employ medications to decrease the risk of capsular contracture in the postoperative setting.

As for emerging technology… If you are planning saline or silicone breast augmentation, an experienced plastic surgeon should discuss the realized risks and benefits of proceeding with surgery vs. waiting five to ten years for newer advancements.

Breast asymmetry: time and managment with implants for augmentation

It often takes 6-9 months for breasts to settle. This can be especially true if interventions to correct pre-existing asymmetry. If I have performed breast asymmetry operations, I will typically ask patients to wait at least 6-9 months before contemplating any revisionary procedures.
