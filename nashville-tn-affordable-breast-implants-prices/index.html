Ask many questions BEFORE you make your appointment and then here are 3 for the consultation.

I think having a good relationship with your surgeon is very important and so are his/her qualifications. Easy questions that you can answer BEFORE even going in are:

1. Is your surgeon certified by The American Board of Plastic Surgery? That is the Board recognized by The American Board of Medical Specialties as the board that certifies plastic surgeons.

Beware of sound-alike Boards or “yes, the doctor is Board-certified.” Always ask Board-certified in what specialty?

2. How long has your doctor been in practice? How long has he/she been doing breast augmentation?

3. Is the operating room facility he/she uses certified by a national agency and is there a board-certified anesthesiologist assigned to your procedure? Most of us list our facility certification on our website.

Otherwise ask the staff when you call. In general, most of our breast augmentation referrals come from happy patients who refer their friends. So ask around, speak with several women who have had breast augmentation and have a list of questions to ask.

At the consultation, ask to see as many examples of results as you think are necessary to have a good discussion.

The 3 questions I would ask at the consultation (after doing the above homeowrk before making an appointment):

  1. How many augmentations have you done? In general, the more the better.
  2. What is the worse complication you have had from breast augmentation?
  3. How often do patients in your practice need revision surgery? What are the financial policies in case a second surgery is needed? Breast augmentation should be a rewarding experience is you do your homework and have realistic expectations.
