Breast Cleavage, Gap and Breast Implants

Breast Implant Surgery in London is much more like Golf than Poker. In Poker the player can ask for another card or two while in Golf the player has to play the condition and terrain he/she is in.

Your particular breast and chest anatomy will largely determine what result you will have. A few facts for you to consider:- Unless you are undergoing a breast lift, your implants need to be located dead center under the location of your nipples.

Doing otherwise will make the implants seem too low / to the side / to the center or too high. – for the implants to be seen as much as possible in frontal (rather than sideways projection) they need to stay on the flat portion of your chest.

Stated another way, your surgeon needs to pick an implant whose base diameter matches the base diameter of your current breast which hopefully is also the span of the more or less plateau area of your chest.

Picking a much larger based implant will result in the all too commonly seen scenario of implants sliding to the sides and “doing battle” with your arms as they move forward. All women have a greater degree than men of keel (boat like) sloping chests.

This builds a natural tendency for implants to go sideways ESPECIALLY if the side pocket is made too large and or large implants are used.

– Attempts to shove large implants by encroaching on the middle depressed area between the breasts always fail.
Occasionally they result in either a WINDOW SHADE Deformity (division of the vertical portion of the muscle causes it to “go up” when contracting giving the breasts a strange appearance) or worse a UNI BOOB DEFORMITY – one pocket with two meeting implants.

Bad scenario – hard to fix. The way to get the best compromise result is to go with your surgeon over your options and pick the one that is best for you based on your examination.

In other words, play the ball where it landed and properly deal with the anatomy you have.
