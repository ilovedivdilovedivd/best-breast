Breast implants in Hawaii – when to do them.

If you are considering breast augmentation and pregnancy in the same sentence then I would advise you to have your children first and then augmentation.

I like to refer to augmentation as a pseudo pregnancy because the breast enlarge with pregnancy.

The skin of the breast can stretch considerably and this can result in skin stretching and breast distortion. If you were to get pregnant with implants already in place, then your skin would stretch more and the stretching and stretch marks would be exaggerated.

The likely-hood of needing a breast lift following a pregnancy is that much greater. In my experience, most woman lose breast tissue following pregnancy.

This is due to the breast being engorged with milk and the extra pressure cause the breast tissue to atrophy (become thinner).

Now if you are going to wait a few years before having children, then I would take my chances and have the surgery in Hawaii. There is no need to waste a few good years of looking great.

I would recommend a Breast Lift with your implants because it will give an overall better result. Yes, you can just do the implant, but they may sag. It also depends on what you are looking for.

If you just want an implant and the lift isn’t your concern, just do the implant. However, I recommend a lift with the implants.

It is hard to say exactly how your breast will look once it drops, but it will take on a more natural contour, with a fullness in the lower portion.

LOOK VS. FEEL

Saline or silicone implants in Hawaii are intrinsically not more or less natural-looking. It is more the feel of the implanted breast that is more natural with silicone when it functions as designed. The look is more related to your natural breast shape and size and the choice of size, dimension, and style, as well as position of the implant(above or below the muscle) rather than the choice of filler material.

If you have a good fit between your breasts and the implant, you can look very natural, or not unnatural, using saline implants. However, the feel might be less natural than the same silicone implants.

It then becomes a trade-off between your comfort with saline or silicone, and the ned to feel more or less natural. And of course, you might not know what they feel like until they are actually in your body.

For Breast Augmentation Use a More Locally Placed Incision Than The Tummy Tuck
The key to a good breast augmentation is control of the inframammary fold and pocket dissection. While it may be possible to place breast implants through the tummy tuck incision, this is not any easy way to achieve those requirements.

Using a high-lateral tension abdominoplasty approach, dissection in the upper abdomen is confined primarily to the central one third, and so the area inferior to each breast is not dissected.

And from an accessibility standpoint, the breast is farther away from the level of dissection in the upper abdomen than you might think.

The inframammary fold incision allows the surgeon to control the level of the fold, provides good exposure for either a subglandular or subpectoral pocket, avoids the contaminated area of the nipple-areolar complex, heals well, is reusable for future operations, and is well hidden in the inframammary crease.
A properly positioned implant, and subsequent happy patient, will more than compensate for the small amount of extra scarring.

Moderate Plus Vs High Profile Silicone Gel Implants

There is definitely a difference in moderate plus compared to high profile implants, although sometimes it’s hard to tell just from the photos. High profile implants give a more dramatic result, with more fullness at the top part of the breast (upper pole).

They’re shaped differently from moderate plus implants, with a narrower base that gives the more pronounced forward projection. Younger women often prefer the high profile implants, as they look more like a natural youthful breast, while the moderate plus profile implants result in a more natural shape for older women.

The bottom line is that results look different on different bodies. You may love the look of high profile implants on other women, yet on yourself the moderate plus choice could be better. An excellent surgeon can help you make the right choice.

Risks Associated with Breast Augmentation

As with any type of surgical procedure, certain risks accompany breast augmentation surgery. It is important to know the risks and discuss them with your doctor so you are prepared in the event a complication occurs.

Side effects such as tenderness, swelling and bruising are normal, but excessive swelling, reddness, vomiting or fever over 101 degrees is of concern and requires immediate medical attention. These are possible signs of infection. To prevent this from occuring, you’re doctor takes extra precautions during surgery and also prescribes antibiotics to take after breast implants surgery in Hawaii.
Other risks include: Rippling – Rippling typically occurs with saline breast implants, particularly when placed over the muscle. This condition is when the edges of the implant are seen under the skin. To reduce this risk, Hawaii implants are placed under the muscle or half under/half over the muscle.

Capsular Contracture – a condition where the protective tissue that is formed around the implant tightens around the implant causing distortion and pain. To help prevent this, your surgeon will create a pocket slightly larger than the implant to allow it extra space.

This also provides extra space incase excess tissue forms. Breast augmentation massage is used to move the implant around in the pocket to prevent this excess tissue from forming, which also keeps breasts soft and natural looking.
Rupture/ Leaking Implants – A very common concern among women is “what happens if my implant leaks?” With saline implants, the affects are quite noticable because the implant will deflate and the saline, which is salt water, will be purged from the body.

Detecting a rupture with silicone implants in Hawaii is a bit more tricky. Because the implant is made in a similar fashion to a gummy bear the contents of the implant do not leak out into they body, however it is important to have it removed and replaced as soon as possible because it may lead to infection.

When an implant ruptures, some patients have reported breast pain, swelling, fever, joint pain and fatigue. If you experience any of these symptoms or notice that your saline implant has deflated, you will need to call your surgeon right away.
