Tampa, FL Breast Augmentation Procedure

Tampa Fl breast implants have been around for over 40yrs and breast augmentation has been a top sought after plastic surgery procedure for years. This procedure is well established and safe – as well as the implants that have been extensively studied.

There is no significant change in the current technology in the near future. Stem cells may some day play a role in breast augmentation, but that is likely decades away. For now feel comfortable that patients who undergo the procedure do very well and enjoy the results.

Breast implants in Tampa FL do not cause weight gain over time. Your breast weight consists of glandular tissue, fat, and implants. The size (weight) of your implants do not change unless there is a rupture. The change in size that occurs with breasts are common to all women.

Glandular tissue increases and decreases in size in response to hormonal changes (pregnancy, aging, etc). The fat tissue in your breasts changes in a similar fashion to fat tissue elsewhere in your body. This is based on metabolism, activity, age, etc.

Rippling risks and breast implants

Visible rippling is more problematic with saline implants rather than silicone implants. The degree to which the rippling is visible is dependant on the amount of body fat and breast tissue you have.

Silicone implants are not dangerous to your health and there are many scientific studies to show that fact

Many will say you will do better with a lift, however you are quite right in that the scar can cause regrets. There is nothing wrong with an implant such as a silicone moderate profile to take up the breast skin and see how things work out.

A lift can be done as a later procedure if you are unhappy with your choice and might be a chance worth taking.

Breast Feeding after augmentation

This is a concern that many women considering augmentation share. Implants typically do not interfere with breast feeding. Most women are able to breast feed after breast augmentation with no difficulty. However, this is based on the assumption that you would have been able to breast feed prior to implants.

Breast feeding with implants should pose no threat to the health of your infant.

First, putting more saline into the implants is another operation. I think that you think this can be done with a needle through the skin. It cannot. The manufacturer does not recommend this approach.

With this approach, at best you can become only a little bit bigger. So, my advice is consider living with the size you have (smaller breasts age better) or just have the implants replaced with larger implants in Tampa.

Gummy Bear Breast Implants vs Saline or Silicone?

Gummy bear breast implants are cohesive silicone gel breast implants that were approved by the FDA in 2006. The older liquid silicone gel implants are no longer available and all new silicon gel implants are cohesive gel.

As others have stated silicone gel implants are thought to feel more natural, have less risk of rupture or deflation, and less chance of folds or ripples or other visible abnormalities. Saline breast implants are a silicone rubber shell filled with saltwater.

They can look and feel very normal but do not feel as real as a silicone gel implant and over the first 10 years have a10% deflation rate. Technically speaking the term “gummy bear” was applied to the first new textured cohesive silicon gel implants that had a textured or rough surface on the outside which made them look like a “gum drop or gummy bear”
These textured implants also come in shaped form. Now the term gummy bear is often applied to any cohesive gel implant including textured and smooth walled round cohesive gel implants. One survey found that 83% experienced board-certified plastic surgeons used smooth round cohesive silicon gel breast implants.

The breast will look better with implants alone, which takes us to over or under placement. Each has advantages and disadvantages. Placement of the implants under the muscle may result in high riding implants or double bubble deformity.

Breast size and implants

From the multitude of answers you se here, you’ll find that there is no ‘right’ answer. Every patient has a different anatomy and it requires different sized implants to obtain the same size breasts.

The ribs may curve outward, or inward, there may be wide or narrow cleavage, the chest circumference may be short or very long. The implant determination depends on the anatomy and desires of each patient.
That being said, 100-150 is too small. You will need between 200 and 250cc’s to obtain the size you desire. I have patients bring in photos of what they want to look like postop. I order several sized implants for the surgery and use a sizer (emply implant that is filled to different volumes during the procedure to evaluate which size and shape best matches the patients desires).

The sizer is removed and then the proper sized implant is placed. with your decision.

Is the Inframammary incision now considered “old school” and dated?

Like a lot of technical issues, without the proper background and understanding it can become confusing. I think you have identified the benefits of this approach in the latter part of your question.

As a somewhat busy breast enhancement surgeon, I feel that the infra-mammary approach allows for the best exposure to the surgeon (and thus predictability in the outcome), lies well hidden in the already present natural crease of the infra-mammary fold, and typically heals very well.

For many surgeons, it is also the “go to” incision in the event that a complication, such as pocket adjustment needs to be performed later on. This is often regardless if an axillary or umbilical incision was initially made.
This is due to the benefit of the enhanced exposure, and thus greater likelihood of getting the job done right in a secondary procedure.
