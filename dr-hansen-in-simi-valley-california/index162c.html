Zoom is a high concentration hydrogen peroxide gel that lifts stain out of natural tooth structure, but does not change the color of dental restorations like bonding, veneers, or crowns.  You should Zoom first, then wait a week for the color to settle, then change the bonding to match your new tooth color.  Good Luck!

Can zoom whitening be bone on just 5 lower front teeth as I have caps and bridge on the other teeth?
Zoom
Apr 7, 2016
Great question!  Zoom is an in office professionally applied bleaching system.  Zoom bleach is a high concentration hydrogen peroxide gel that can burn gums or soft tissues in the mouth, and damage porcelain restorations such as crowns or veneers.  For each Zoom patient, a trained technition must place customized barriers to block out the gumline as well as any teeth that are not needed to be bleached.  Zoom can be aplied to as many or few teeth as desired.  My best to you!
