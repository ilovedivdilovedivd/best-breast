Breast Augmentation in Charleston is a Surgical Service, Not a Commodity.

There is a wide variation from one community to another, or region to another, or from one surgeon to another, as to the surgical fee for Breast Augmentation or any other cosmetic surgical procedure, for that matter.

A multitude of factors are involved in this price disparity and lack of “standardization”. When it comes to Breast Augmentation, the only “fixed” cost is the cost of the implants in Charleston. This is relatively constant from one doctor to another, and from one region to another. Silicone gel implants are about twice the cost of saline implants, and cost is not related to size of the implants.

Breast implant costs in NY

However, the implant is only one component of the “global surgical fee”, which includes not only the implants, but the cost of the facility fee where the surgery is done, the anesthesia fee, any additional supplies, and the surgeon’s professional fee.

These “variable” costs can span a wide spectrum, from seemingly “bargain basement” fees, to very expensive “Park Avenue” or “Beverly Hills” prices. Patients need to consider the factors above before being shocked by the surgical fee, and also before choosing the lowest possible fee.

Questions to ask yourself include: What is the level of experience of the doctor? What kind of facility will provide my surgery and what is the office environment in which I will receive my follow-up care?

How much attention will I receive after surgery? How accessible is the doctor? What is the cost of doing business in one state or community vs. another? Am I willing to pay a premium for a greater level of service, or do I want only the lowest price? As in most purchases, you get what you pay for. While it is possible to find an occasional bargain on an item of high value, or to over-pay for something that in the end is not quite “worth it”, there is almost always a “sweet-spot” where value and price intersect. Hopefully, this happens when you find the surgeon with whom you are comfortable, who you trust to provide you excellent care. Remember, you’re not buying a rug or a bushel of wheat, you are choosing surgery. Shop carefully, but remember, you’re buying not just the steak, but the sizzle as well! (Athleo Louis Cambre, MD, Los Angeles Plastic Surgeon)

It is important to understand that implants are not intended to be lifelong devices. I make it clear to all my patients that every implant will eventually leak.

I have seen this occur as early as two years and have also witnessed patients whose implants are still intact at 20.

Charleston saline implants are very apparent when they leak since the fluid is quickly absorbed by the body and the breast decreases in size.

This does not occur with the silicone gel implants as the filler material (silicone) tends to stay contained with in its implant shell.

Should you have a silicone implant, signs of leakage may include changes to the appearance or feel of the implant or a thickening of the surrounding scar envelope called the capsule.

Other signs such as a lump or discomfort may also be an indication of an implant leak. It should be comforting to know, however, that the safety profile on silicone gel implants is good.

Should these signs occur, it is recommended that you are evaluated by a board-certified plastic surgeon. (Melek Kayser, MD, Detroit Plastic Surgeon)

When should breast implants be replaced?

Saline implants don’t need to be replaced until the patient decides that she is no longer satisfied with the way they look or feel. If there is a major leak, it will be obvious and the implant can be replaced.

If there is a very gradual leak, this doesn’t matter until the look or feel of the implant changes enough to warrant a replacement surgery.

Remember that it’s only water that is leaking, so if you aren’t unhappy with the implants, don’t worry about slow, gradual leaks. Some saline implants have problems and need to be replaced within a year, while others may last for decades.

Silicone implants in Charleston are a little more difficult to assess. A silicone implant may leak without any change in the size or appearance of the breast, since the silicone is still there. Many physicians feel that silicone implants don’t need to be changed until the look or feel is unsatisfactory.

According to the FDA, which regulates implants as well as medications, an MRI of the breasts should be performed every other year starting in year 3. An MRI can determine whether silicone has leaked outside the shell of the implant. If there is leakage on the MRI, the implants can be replaced. As with saline implants, silicone implants can have problems within a year, or may last for decades.

In your specific case, it would likely be reasonable to replace your implants in Charleston. If you feel air pockets, pain, etc. A revision surgery could be helpful.

There’s no guarantee that replacing the implants will improve the pain or rash, but it’s probably worthwhile to try at this point. A board-certified plastic surgeon can evaluate you in person and help you to devise a logical plan. (Andres Taleisnik, MD, Orange County Plastic Surgeon)

It’s not usually the implant that makes us reoperate

As several other PS’s have stated below, there is no expiration date on breast implants. In the past, when previous generations of implants had higher rupture or leakage rates, a good rule of thumb was to at least have your implants re-evaluated at 10 years. I still think that’s a good idea, especially if you haven’t seen your plastic surgeon in many years. I like to see my breast implant patients every two years just to keep track of them and make sure they are not developing any problems. The real issue is that it’s not usually the implant that will cause us to reoperate, but rather the aging of the patient, life changes (babies), weight fluctuations, loss of skin elasticity (sagging skin), and other things that just go along with getting older. Some patients just hold up better than others for a variety of reasons, and in these cases, there’s no reason to replace breast implants just because of a certain time limit. (Simeon Wall, Jr, MD, FACS, Shreveport Plastic Surgeon)

Breast implant sizes, Volume is not the right number

Breast implants in Charleston come in variety of sizes. Usually people refer to them by volume but there are several other ways to classify implant types which are more useful. The implant width is probably the most important factor to know when determining an particular implant for an individual.

The second most important thing is the profile. The profile refers to how much the implant sticks out from the chest. The higher the profile the more unnatural (stripper) the implant will look.

The profiles are, from low to high, style 10 (moderate), style 15 (moderate plus), style 20 (high), style 45 (ultra high). The breast width is influenced the amount of breast tissue naturally there and the chest width (bra size 34-50).

This is a number your board certified physician can measure and tell you at the time of a consultation. Once this number is known then the profile is picked based on the desired look. Probably the biggest source of revisions that I do are from people who had implants placed by another surgeons and the implant is too narrow.

An example of this can be seen on my website under breast revisions. (Chad K. Wheeler, MD, Spokane Plastic Surgeon)

Choosing the right size breast implant for you is one of the most important aspects of a consultation.
