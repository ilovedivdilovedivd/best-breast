Tilted premolars and buccal corridor. What are my options to fix this?
Movement of teeth
May 15, 2020
Hi there. Just so you know, teeth are always moving. Unless you wear an appliance at night that will hold them in place such as a retainer or night guard, the daily forces placed upon teeth will cause them to shift constantly.  Based on the information you provided, Invisalign (or even other orthodontic treatments) would be a better option to get the desired alignment that you would like.
I am having gum graft for recession of gums, no periodontal disease. Should I have Invisalign done
invisalign and gum graft
Feb 15, 2020
Typically, you want the teeth to be moved first and then gum grafting can be done. Additionally, for patients that have recession or thin tissue, we recommend the teeth not be moved forward, that instead they are moved backward slightly.  This is something you should definitely discuss with your periodontist and orthodontist prior to any treatment.
