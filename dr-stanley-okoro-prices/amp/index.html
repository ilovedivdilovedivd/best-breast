How long do breast implants last

Breast implants are not permanent devices, they are long-term devices. They are expected to last about 10 years, but if you are not having any problems, you don’t necessarily have to do anything.

Breast implant surgery can be done day-only

Breast implant surgery is normally performed as a day-only procedure – whether the patient is having a full general anaesthetic or some sort of twilight sedation. Every plastic surgeon does things slightly differently, so it’s important to understand from your PS why they would want you to stay in overnight.

In my practice, all implant surgery is performed under a general anaesthetic on an outpatient (day-only) basis. I use lots of local anaesthetic, so the patients wake up pain-free and go home with tablet pain-killers.

I normally see them the day after their surgery to make sure everything is ok.

Saline can look & feel very natural!

Saline and silicone implants both have pro’s and con’s. (One thing that may be viewed as a “con” by one patient is a “pro” to another, such as being able to immediately know of rupture in the case of saline implants due to noticeable breast deflation.)

Saline implants have a slightly higher rupture rate, *can* look and feel less natural and are more likely to show a rippling appearance than silicone. But that doesn’t mean there aren’t patients that have wonderfully natural looking saline augmentations! One thing I can reassure you is the safety of both types of implants.
When placed under the muscle, you are going to have the best result with either. I would do some more reading, look at as many outcomes as you can and also review the warranties from the companies that make the implants you are considering.

I’ve provided some info in my link below and on that site there are more links to the FDA’s site and implant manufacturers.

Breast implants vs. Fat Grafting

Fat grafts are loaded with stem cells, whether you specially process the fat or not. In fact, fat tissue contains larger volumes of stem cells than the traditional source — bone marrow. Fat grafting to the breast was abandoned, when breast implants became available because the results were better.

Breast implants were safer and more effective. Fat grafting cannot add the same volume that an implant can. Fat grafting can lead to calcium deposits in the breast, making mammography difficult to read, so the grafts should not be placed deep in the breast tissue, only superficially, making nodules more easily palpable.
Interesting work is being done in the field of stem cell/fat grafting, but it is by no means mainstream or accepted as a reasonable alternative to breast implants in 2011. Breast implants are the most investigated medical device know to man, so it is unlikely that any new problems will be found with them.

While breast implants are not perfect, but they are the best thing we have available. When I started my practice in 1997, fat grafting to the breast was one of the few procedures specifically excluded from my malpracatice policy.

Those performing the procedure should check this for their patients sake. Though we didn’t know it in the 90’s, the stems cells were already in our fat grafts. Many of the problems with the original fat grafting are not likely to be solved by adding more stems cells.
More research is necessary. Additionally, while the problems with breast implants are know, the long term results of artificially increasing the number of stem cells in the graft are unknown.

Breast cancer and breast implants – Should I get breast implants with a family history

Breast implants will not impart a greater risk of breast cancer. However, once you perform the breast implantation, you should be remain vigilant with breast cancer screening.

Also, you may consider genetic testing if you are very concerned about breast cancer.

What Type of Implant Will Make my Breasts Look/Feel More Natural?

For the person with not much breast tissue, and I think you fall into that category, a silicone gel implant will certainly feel more natural, and depending on the size of implant, will likely look more natural.

Dr Stanley Okoro Is A Double Board Certified Plastic Surgeon

Saline implants tend to project their feel and shape much more readily through your breast tissue, hence they feel and often look less natural. Because U. S. surgeons for almost 25 years were only able to use saline implants for cosmetic breast augmentation, we became accustomed to the look of saline implants, which can look overly round and unnaturally full in the upper pole.

Gel implants overall tend to have a softer and more natural appearance. For the individual with very little breast tissue, one of the new generation of tear drop shaped implants will provide the most natural appearance.

In this patient group with minimal breast tissue, even a softer round gel implant can still look somewhat round. In part it is a matter of personal preference and some women prefer the slightly rounded appearance of the upper pole that a round implant tends to create, and again would be most evident in someone with little breast tissue.

When there is more breast tissue covering the implant the difference would be more subtle. All shaped implants have a textured surface so that they adhere to your tissues and hence prevent them rotating which would of course not look right.

Dr Stanley Okoro Plastic Surgery

Creating the implant pocket for this type of implant must done carefully so that implant fits snugly within the pocket. When done properly the incidence of implant rotation is vanishing small. I have never had a patient develop implant rotation in my practice and the latest study from Sientra with thousands of patients showed a zero incidence of rotation.

To imply that shaped implants have a significant incidence of rotation and result in a deformed breast shape is simply wrong. Also, it has been commonly recognized for years, and several large studies have demonstrated that textured surface implants have a lower rate of capsular contracture, particularly if they are placed in the sub glandular (on top of muscle) position.

If implants are placed in the sub muscular (under the muscle), then the incidence of capsular contracture is about the same for smooth and textured surface implants. There are no well done studies or other data to support the claim that textured surface implants have a higher rate of capsular contracture.
