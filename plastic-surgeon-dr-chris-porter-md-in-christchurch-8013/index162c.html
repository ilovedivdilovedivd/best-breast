Will a breast augmentation cause me any additional back problems?
Back problems
Nov 9, 2020
Any extra weight added to your bust can potentially create postural issues and back pain. If you do proceed I would recommend an implant at the smaller end of the range. Once you are healed then it is fine to undertake massage or chiropractor treatments. All the best.

Best option
Nov 9, 2020
With your characteristics and desired look I would recommend a round implant in front of the muscle. From the photos I would doubt that you need a lift, best to see a Board Certified Plastic Surgeon who specialises in cosmetic breast surgery. All the best.
Is breast augmentation permanent?
Breast implants are not “permanent”
Nov 9, 2020
Breast implants are not to be considered lifetime devices, it is likely that most women who have had implants for breast augmentation will require a revisional procedure at some stage. However, implants do not have to be removed at a particular time. Generally if you are happy and comfortable then you do not need the implants to be revised.
All the best.
I think my right nipple may be necrotizing after breast lift. Is this normal?
Nipple necrosis
May 23, 2019
I agree with you that your right nipple has a degree of necrosis. This is an unfortunate complication of breast lift surgery. I advise you to keep in close contact with your Plastic Surgeon until it fully heals. Hopefully this occurs with dressings alone but sometimes further surgery is required. Be wary of infection, if there is redness around the wound or you start to feel hot and unwell then seek urgent medical attention preferably from your surgeon. All the best.
Is it possible to just raise the nipples without a breast lift?
Nipple lift
May 23, 2019
Yes this is possible. The technique depends on the degree of lift you want – a small lift could be achieved with a crescentic lift and a more moderate lift with a peri-areolar approach. Either of these will involve removing a small amount of skin only and preserve your breast volume and shape.
Can doctors “anchor” the tissue internally when performing breast lifts so they are less likely to sag
Breast lift internal anchor
May 23, 2019
It is possible to internally anchor breast tissue to reduce future sagging, however even with these techniques there is a slow sag with time. Before embarking on a second lift you will need to find out the details of the first operation to inform the next surgeon, in order to minimise risk and optimise your shape. All the best.
Is 28 days of being nicotine-free safe for breast reduction surgery?
Nicotine free
May 23, 2019
I recommend to all my patients that they need to be nicotine free for 4 weeks prior to surgery and 4 weeks following surgery. This will minimise your wound healing, infection and anaesthetic risks significantly. All the best.
