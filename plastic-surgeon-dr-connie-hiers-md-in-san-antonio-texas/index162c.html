Statement: Dr. Connie Hiers is a board-certified plastic surgeon who brings over 36 years of experience with both surgical and nonsurgical procedures to her practice in San Antonio, Texas. She aims to help you achieve the confidence that comes from being the most beautiful you.

After graduating from Texas Tech University with a degree in microbiology, Dr. Hiers earned her medical degree from the University of Texas Health Science Center at Houston. She continued her postgraduate training with a general surgery residency at the University of Hawaii. She was then accepted to a plastic surgery fellowship at the University of Tennessee in Chattanooga, where she received specialized training in burn wound care and microvascular surgery.

Dr. Hiers served as chief of surgery at Methodist Hospital in Jonesboro, Arkansas, before starting her private practice in Jonesboro, making her the first plastic surgeon in Northeast Arkansas to perform cosmetic and reconstructive plastic surgery. After 30 years in Jonesboro, she returned to her native Texas to open her San Antonio practice, Dr. Connie Hiers Plastic Surgery & Med Spa.

Dr. Hiers is board certified by the American Board of Plastic Surgery and a fellow of the American College of Surgeons. She is also a member of several professional societies, including the American Society of Plastic and Reconstructive Surgery, the American Society of Plastic Surgeons, the American Society of Laser Surgery and Medicine, the Southern Medical Association, and the Texas Medical Association.

What is the mortality rate for liposuction under general anesthesia?
mortality rate
Nov 20, 2023
The mortality rate is lower than driving to the hospital to have surgery done. The risk for both liposuction and general anesthesia is low in healthy people, and even together, is under 1%. For general anesthesia is 1 per 100,000 and for liposuction is 19 per 100,000. The risks increase in those with medical issues and obesity and smoking.
Is liposuction possible on the face?
liposuction
Nov 20, 2023
You already have a slim face. Liposuction is not done routinely on the face because of the risk of nerve damage. It can be done under the chin safely to remove excess fat.
Age 38, 140lbs, risks of fat embolism during or after liposuction with regional anesthesia?
liposuction
Nov 20, 2023
The risk of fat emboli with liposuction is very low since fat is being sucked out rather than blown into tissue. Plus, blood vessels are left intact with liposuction. This is higher risk with a tummy tuck since it is more invasive, especially with blood and not fat emboli. Precautions will be used in surgery to decrease your risk. Fat emboli are more associated with fat transfers than liposuction.
