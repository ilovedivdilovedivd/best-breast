Best option for breast implant placement

Options for placement of breast implants include: IMF (the breast fold) Periareolar (around the pigmented portion surrounding your nipple) Transaxillary (under your arm) Belly Button (through the BB) And, there are two main surgical planes: Above the muscle Below the muscle So how do you decide which approach is best for you? It really depends.

First, the belly button approach is generally frowned upon by most plastic surgeons. If you look at the bulk of surgeons placing implants this way, they are generally not plastic surgeons and are looking at this as a marketing opportunity (e.g.: Look at this new way of placing the implant).

The problem is that this is also an approach where you have the least control over accurate placement of the implant. As such, most well-trained plastic surgeons do not offer this option.

Next, the peri-areolar approach can be an option but is much less popular these days because of evidence pointing to a higher long-term risk for capsular contracture.

In addition, unless the scar heals remarkably well, the scar is on the most obvious portion of your breast.

Finally, the transaxillary approach can be a valid option. The problem is that you also lose a good deal of control over implant placement through this route. In addition, it is much harder to place the pre-filled silicone gel implants this way.

With these considerations in mind, I generally prefer placement of a silicone gel implant through the IMF approach beneath the muscle.

I see great results overall, my patients are happy, and I have very low incidence of capsular contracture.

Mechanical maneuvering of breast implant position almost never works.

When breast implants are properly positioned, they will ultimately assume a natural shape as the swelling that may artificially prop up the implants subsides. This may take as long as several months, getting better as the days pass.

Implant placement vis-a-vis exercise

I recommend placing the implants partially or fully under the muscle. In a fit patient, this avoids the “edge” feeling–I am assuming that you have a low BMI and are thin. Implants do not move and this is not a consideration.

In any case, you should refrain from exercise for several weeks following surgery subject to your plastic surgeon’s recommendations.

Breast implant settling and size

depending upon your degree of swelling right now, your implants may seem a little smaller as they settle into place, which usually occurs within the first 6 months after surgery. They generally do not appear to be larger after this period.

However, 450 cc is a fairly large implant when used in combination with a breast lift, so based on your stated size now, you should certainly be able to achieve a large C or small D with the current implants (depending upon the specific bra manufacturer, of course).
