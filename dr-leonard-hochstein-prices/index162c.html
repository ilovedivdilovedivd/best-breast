Dr Leonard Hochstein Prices

Size implants for breast augmentation

Determining correct implant size is dependent on many factors besides height and weight, such as patient goals, and skin laxity.

In general, I recommend smaller than 300cc for my size 0-2 patients. Experience helps in these situations and in my 30 years of experience that is what I recommend.

How many days are needed to recovery after breast implants will depend in many factors, pain tolerance will depend also on personal variables, in general patients will require narcotic pain medication, most patients around 7-10 days, of course if you are taking narcotic pain medication, you should not drive and you should not work in a job where you need to make important decisions, because you are under the influence of narcotics.

Now, are you going to be able to be off narcotic in 3-4 days, as some answers suggest?, most likely not, if you start driving while on narcotics, legally, if you are involved in an accident you will be DUI.

Because of this, I usually recommend patients to be off work, as much as they can be. Some patients are able to be back to work, really early, but some need 3-4 weeks (rare), if you can get 3 weeks off, but you are ready to be back at 5 days, its usually easy to do, the other way is usually more difficult.

To finish, no body,can predict the future, so at the end you have to consider all the options. The most important factor in obtaining a good result from your cosmetic surgery is the surgeon you elect to perform it, be sure you seek the help of a surgeon member of The American Society for Aesthetic Plastic Surgery, this will assure you of the proper training and credentials of your siurgeon.
There is no fool-proof way of determining the right “partner” in your venture. Looking good “on paper” doesn’t mean it’ll work in reality although basic things like board-certification in plastic surgery is a good place to start.

Of the most important things, I would put at the top of the list are:

1. Ask what his or her philosophy is regarding breast augmentation. That is, not the technique, but what guides the decision-making process.

If a surgeon always does things the same way, that philosophy is different from someone who varies or customizes the procedure. How does the philosophy determine technique?

2. Ask for references from previous patients and perhaps even from your own doctor or gynecologist.

They often know the reputation of good surgeons. 3. See how much time and energy the surgeon personally spends discussing your case. See if they shuttle you to a surgical consultant or tells you what to do in 5-10 minutes without knowing you.
Often it is a gut feeling if you trust or don’t trust someone.

Candidate for breast implants?

You are a perfect candidate for breast augmentation surgery. Breast implants simply push out and fill out what you are starting with. Right now your breasts are very attractive, they are symmetric, and you have no sagging.

A modest sized implant will make your breasts a larger version of what they are now. If you choose an implant that is too big or too wide you will lose some of the pleasing aspects of your current breast appearance.

You need to research breast photos to figure out what type of look you are trying to obtain and then convey that information to your surgeon by reviewing the photos and using sizers in your bra.
The satisfaction rate of breast augmentation patients is very high. I am sure you will be thrilled with your result. If you pick an experienced board-certified plastic surgeon to perform your augmentation an excellent result is virtually assured.

Augmentation and pregnancy

During pregnancy with a breast augmentation your breasts will become enlarged just as they would if you did not have an augmentation. Because the skin on the breasts will stretch during the pregnancy, you may see stretch marks and you may find that your breasts are saggy.

I have not had any patients tell me that there was any pain associated with breast implants and their pregnancies.

Breast augmentation without a lift?

it appears that you would receive the greatest benefit from a breast lift with smaller implants. 750cc implants may fill up loose breast skin; my concern is that you might end up with big droopy breasts instead of full youthful appearing breasts.
Probably not possible to replace implants through armpits

If you have a significant capsule that needs even partial removal or want silicone implants, you are probably better off going through the areola or under the breast.

Avoid Breast Lift

I believe you need a lift with an implant. I know that’s probably not what you wanted to hear, but it will give you the result you want.

There is too much sagging (ptosis) of the breasts to avoid a lift, and I think you’ll need an implant to replace the volume loss. For my thinner patients that need a lift, I would recommend a smooth gel implant.

Breast lift

A Breast lift procedure would be best to improve the appearance ofyour breast. For you to get in an aestheticallypleasing breast a lift is indicated otherwise your nipple will be lower than your breast implant position.
Wait at least 3 months after breast feeding before pursuing a breast augmentation

Typically I advise our patients to wait at least three months after breast feeding before proceeding with a breast augmentation procedure. This is to allow time for the breast glandular tissue to settle down from lactation, for the gland to reach its ultimate post partum size, and for the breast tissue to decrease in vascularity.

Large Implants

It has not been unnoticed that this is your second post and you are wondering if it’s ‘OK’ to get 650 cc implants. Based on the responses from both posts it should be clear that although a physical exam is necessary before making recommendations, it is highly unlikely that implants of that size will do you well.
Summary of most doctor’s posts:

1. Get sized for the implant based on your anatomy (just like buying shoes).
2. Avoid periareolar entry of implant.
3. Less important: breast lift for optimum aesthetics, silicone gel, and avoid high profile.

The breast that is droopier is at risk for ‘double bubble’ with an implant that is too large or with a surgeon who tries too aggressively to ‘lift’ the breast with an implant alone. This is very common and women have posted on this very issue after the fact many times on Realself.com.

Please take these recommendations seriously. If the doctor you’re seeing is willing to put in 650 cc implants, you should see a few others for second opinions, preferably specialists in all cosmetic breast surgery, not just implants.
If you downsize, you may need a breast lift

Mariana, In reference to your previous question, if you downsize the implant moderately, then you may actually have a more natural appearing breast. If you downsize significantly, then you may actually need a breast lift to accommodate for the extra skin.

If you are just having an implant exchange, the surgery is usually straight-forward.

Transaxillary breast augmentation

It is possible to get beautiful results with this incision, however you should find someone with experience. Malpositioning is a common concern and you need someone that has experience to improve your surgical outcome.

Implant profile

I agree that you should take some goal pictures to your surgeon. Typically you will get more projection with the high profile style implant. The moderate plus style typically will give you less drastic upper pole fullness.
In addition to goal pictures I would suggest that you try on sizers. Ideally you want an implant that fits the measurements of your breast!

Drop and Fluff

Breast implants can definitely make the breasts look larger as they “drop and fluff’ which basically means the skin relaxes and allows the implants to settle.
