Double Bubble After Breast Augmentation

The “double bubble” deformity isn’t something you, as a patient, can prevent. Life, genetics and gravity work against the female breasts to continue to age them. After augmentation these factors continue to age the breasts.
The result can be a sagging of the breast. The breast implant will remain in its surgically created position due to the capsule that forms around it. Anatomically it really can’t move. So the breast descends.

This, well, is unfortunate because the implant remains in the correct position and the breast falls below it creating the famous “double bubble.” The only way to correct this is to lift the breast back on to the implant via a mastopexy or breast lift.

So don’t fret. If it happens, it happens and it will need to be corrected. But you may have good genes and your breasts will continue to look great for years.

Massage following Breast Implant surgery

I typically will have all of my breast augmentation patients scheduled for a lymphatic massage at 7 days post-operative in my comprehensive Medical Spa. Our certified massage therapist will teach the patient techniques that I would like them to continue to do in order to help reduce swelling and maintain a nice soft capsule which naturally forms around the implant.
