Breast implants without a lift will make matters worse

Breast implants can produce a satisfactory result if there is modest ptosis (sagging) present. For women who have fairly pronounced ptosis with the nipple at or below the inframammary crease (the crease below the breast), placing implants alone will give a very undesirable appearance with the breast tissue falling off the implants.

You will be happiest with implants and a Breast lift. The cost is more and more incisions are required, but there is no other way to obtain the right shape.

NEVER chase drooping with a bigger implant- that is of paramount importance and a HUGE mistake. You have beautiful breasts that can be even prettier in the right hands.

I 100% agree with the two plastic surgeons that told you that you need alift in order to get the best result. You look like a great candidate for breast augmentation. I recommend getting a consult, as a physical exam will give the most information.

Not too old

Health status is more important than age in terms of surgery. You certainly seem healthy and your age is not considered too old, you should have a good result with a natural look that lasts for many years with the right approach.

Breast Augmentation for Superior Fullness

A breast Augmentation without a lift will not achieve your desired result. In fact, since you like the size of your breasts, the most reasonable option is a lift without implants.

Implants would make your already large breasts look “fake,” one thing you do not want.
They would also make the droop you have even worse.

Breast augmentation without lift

I think you would have a very nice result with implants alone, no lift needed. Your breast position relative to your pectoralis muscle and inframammary crease appears to be good, but your breast tissue has deflated with your weight loss.

Implants are a great way to replace lost volume and rejuvenate the breast.

Implants or breast lift?

I think you’d be a great candidate for an augmentation to give you upper pole fullness. Best to see a board certified plastic surgeon who takes the time to properly measure and evaluate you to determine your best implant size.

Breast augmentation and breast feeding

Often, patients will ask if breast feeding is ok after breast augmentation. It’s important to know that not only does the American Society of Plastic Surgeons recommend that mothers attempt breast feeding after breast augmentation but we think that it is not really affected at all.

Silent rupture dilemma

The controversy regarding systemic auto-immune diseases being caused by silicone gel breast implants have been adequately put to rest. They don’t. If you are suggesting that silicone was detected in your axillary lymph nodes and thus led to or confirmed the diagnosis of ruptured implants, then it is not necessary to remove the nodes themselves if they are not inflamed.

The difficult question is what to do with intracapsular (gel remains inside the natural capsule of scar tissue) silent (non-symptomatic) ruptures. Most doctors, and certainly lawyers, will say to replace them since the implants are not functioning as designed.

The problem is, not all intracapsular silent ruptures will amount to anything — that is, they might not cause any clinical symptoms and remeain aesthetically acceptable and soft. So how can you justify doing surgery on every such implant when the patient has nothing tangible to gain? To complicate matters, we surgeons worry about extra-capsular leakage where the gel gets outside the capsule and starts to migrate through tissue planes.
This is something we don’t want to have happen and can require messy, deforming surgery to treat. All extra-capsular leaks starts as intra-capsular leaks but we can’t tell which intra-capsular leaks will progress to the more serious extra-capsular leaks.

If you decide not to have the implants replaced, be sure to monitor the shape and consistency of the breasts and notify your surgeon if you notice any change.

A Periareolar Lift is a BAD Choice in a woman with Deflated Breasts and Large Areolae

I am sure you are familiar with the Tennis term “running around your backhand”; some players with a poor back hand try unsuccessfully avoiding hitting a backhand by twisting their bodies to try and respond with a forehand hit.

They always lose. And so will you by insisting on a periareolar lift. The periareolar lift works well ONLY in women with minimal sagging whose areolae do not take up so much of the breast surface.

Dr Hedden Breast Augmentation

The periareolar lift, if you think about it, is analogous to fixing a hole in a roof by pulling the tiles around the holes in. Not only will it never look good with a large hole but it will always flatten an object we want to keep cone shaped.

In your case you would do MUCH better with a HALL-FINDLAY BREAST LIFT (aka short scar / vertical scar breast lift). It does involve a lollipop scar BUT the procedures cones your breast tissue and gives you long lasting perky breasts NOT the flat, soon to sag, breasts with sunburst scars around the areola of a periareolar lift taken past its limits.

Of course your implants will “drop”. Gravity is constant. You can determine your size by trying on bras after your swelling has resolved, which will be at least 6 weeks. Your surgeon or his/her office staff can answer all of these questions for you.
