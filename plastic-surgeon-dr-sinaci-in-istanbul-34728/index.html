At what week post-op are you usually “in the clear” without having to worry about wound reopening
depends on the healing
Jun 24, 2023
Hello, there is no certain timing for this but usually when I see my patients at the end of the first week I feel comfortable about this only IF the blood circulation is well. But If you are worried about the wound re-opening months later, probably that will not require an aggressive attempt and will heal spontaneously. Most often suture reactions cause this. Regards.
Ethibond – do you avoid it in your practice?
more than one option
Jun 24, 2023
Hello, during all kind of surgeries including tummy tuck we have lots of suture choices. Ethibond is one of the permanent sutures which has more risk for foreign body reaction and infection. But sometimes we use also ethibond. For example in my routine of tummy tuck I use PDS sutures, that is not permanent and has less risk for foreign body and infection but still has risk. Regards.
