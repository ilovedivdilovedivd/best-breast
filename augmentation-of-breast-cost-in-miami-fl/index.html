A breast augmentation in Miami will increase the size of the breasts. It does not necessarily correct any asymmetries or the general look of your breasts. For example, if your breasts are spaced wide apart on your chest, they will still be wide but they will be larger.

Similarly, if one nipple is higher before surgery, it will likely be a little higher after surgery (provided you are only getting an augmentation). If down the road you decide to have your implants removed your breasts will return to their natural size.

If you had very large implants that stretched out your skin over many years, then you might need a surgery to lift and reposition your nipple and breast tissue once your implants are out.

A saline or silicone breast implant in Miami will enlarge the entire surface area of the breast. Anatomically shaped implants expand the upper pole the least, traditional round silicone implants will increase he upper pole volume moderately and saline implants will increase the upper pole volume the most.

Your concern appears to be focused on the upper pole and I agree that you do not need a lift. For isolated upper pole filling without increasing the entire surface area of the breast I would recommend fat grafting.

This would involve liposuction from some other part of your body which would then be transplanted to the area of concern for a permanent enhancement. Fat grating in breast augmentation is not the best technique for most patients but for someone who has goals such as yourself is a good option.
Detection of rupture is easier for saline vs silicone implants

The decision whether to get saline vs silicone implants is a personal one. Many patients believe silicone implants look and feel more natural than saline ones. If you develop a leak in a saline implant, you will notice it with in hours as one breast will become markedly smaller than the other.

The saline is harmless and is resorbed by your body. You will hover, need to have your implant replaced. Rupture of a silicone filled implant is much harder to detect as the breast shape may not change.

If there is a concern, your plastic surgeon can order an MRI to detect a possible rupture. If the MRI confirms a rupture, you will need to have your implant replaced. The silicone usually remains contained within the breast capsule and causes no significant problems.
Memory gel implants are made of a cohesive silicone gel which does not leak but insted tkes on the consistency of jello. These are the next generation of breast implants to be made available to the public.

The chance of a silicone gel filled implant rupture is approximately 1%/year for the first 6 years. The FDA and the implant companies are still gathering long term data. I have used silicone implants on many patients and they have been very happy with their results.

It may be silent, but there are ways to detect a problem.

Gel implant failures are different with the memory or cohesive gels than with previous implants. Unlike a saline implant which just deflates with a rupture, the gel implants may not change shape at all. The old “liquid” gel implants would deform or become mis-shaped.
The new memory gel implants are designed not to leak as a liquid. The shell material is exactly the same as a saline implant, but does not have the filling valve. There is about a 1% chance over the 1st 5years of an augmentation that an implant may leak.

The current FDA recommendation is for regular physician exams to evaluate the implants. It is also recommended, not required that a patient with a silicone implant have an MRI study after 3-5 years. This is the most sensitive test to evaluate the integrity of the implant and shell.

If the MRI shows a “rupture” or shell abnormality, it is recommended that the implant be replaced. Both Allergan and Mentor have warranty programs which your doctor should imform you about. Their programs provide replacement implants and up to $2600 in reimbursement in the future for an implant failure.
Even if there is a silent leak, it should be contained within the scar capsule, and should be of no danger to you or your breast. The old myth that implants have to be replaced at 10 years has gone by the way side.

“If it ain’t broke, don’t fix it.” I have many patients with implants in place for more than 15-18 years with no problem. They see me or their primary physician yearly for a good breast exam and have regular mammograms if their age is appropriate. The new implants in Miami are excellent and I would recommend the gels to almost all of my patients.

Breast Implants for Petite Women

Breast augmentation in Miami FL is very common in petite women as they commonly do not have large natural breasts. The implants chosen are often < 300cc, and although this may seem small, it provides a dramatic size increase without appearing "unnatural." Many times, the implants are placed under the muscle to "hide" the implant and decrease and rippling or visibility of the implant (especially in the upper portion/cleavage area of the breast). The choice of a moderate versus high profile is a combination of your anatomy (breast width) and your desired size. The high profile allows for more volume for the same width of implant. One important point to note - although the cup size will increase for your breasts, the chest circumference (the 26) will not increase regardless of implant size. SVT and Breast Implants

A history of SVT is not a contraindication to breast augmentation. Neither is celiac disease. The concern about anesthesia however is warranted. For a typical 30 year old woman without a history or cardiac issues, clearance performed by your internist is generally enough.
However, with your significant cardiac history for abnormal rapid heartbeats, clearance for anesthesia is recommended from your cardiologist who oversees your condition. In addition, it is a good idea to meet with or speak to your anesthesiologist preoperatively to discuss the anesthetic risks.

Options in Breast Augmentation Scars

Scars from breast augmentation in Miami will generally be well-hidden in most swimsuits. Scarring is unfortunately an unavoidable byproduct of surgery. There are three most common approaches to breast augmentation:

1. Inframammary incision: The incision is placed in or near the fold at the bottom of the breast.
This approach is probably the most common incision in breast augmentation. It allows for excellent exposure to place the implant above or below the muscle. It also allows for easier access for the surgeon during revision surgery (which will be necessary in almost 100% of breast augmentation cases since implants are NOT lifetime devices).

The scar generally heals very well and is hidden under the bottom crease of the breast.

2. Periareolar incision: The incision is placed at the junction between the areola and the skin of the breast. This approach also allows easy access for implant placement and most revision cases of breast augmentation as well.

The scar heals very well and is hidden in all types of swimsuits.

3. Transaxillary incision: The incision is placed near the armpit area. Therefore, there is no scar on the breast at all. Visualization is a little bit more difficult for the surgeon in placing the implant.

It is also a difficult approach in revision surgery. The scar usually heals well but will not be covered in a swimsuit. As with any procedure, make sure you are well-informed and you reach a comfortable decision with your plastic surgeon.

Incisions for Breast Augmentation

There are several incisions available for breast augmentations. The most common is under the breast (the inframammary fold). This incision is approximately an inch and a half. When placed properly, this should fall exactly in the breast crease and not be noticably, especially in a bra or bikini.
The next most common is around the nipple. This scar is certainly not noticeable in clothing, and can even be hard to notice without clothes if performed properly. However, you must have large enough areolas (the brown pigment).

The armpit is another inicision that can be imperceptible if performed properly, however getting a good position of the implant can be difficult. The belly button incision is limited to saline implants in Miami only.

Breast Implant Sizes

When it comes to determining the best size for you, I would recommend several things. Take some different size implants (you can ask your plastic surgeon for some samples) and place the inside your bra.

Then look in the mirror and see what size you like as this will provide you with a gross estimate of the right amount of cc’s for you. Also, take some magazine/internet pictures of breasts that you like to your plastic surgeon.
This way, both of you will get a good idea of how big you want to be.

Choice of implant size is art and science

All of the measurements you provide are helpful in determining what type of implant will work best, but the size choice is very subjective. Assuming you have tried on implant sizers and 300 cc is what you like, then the high profile is the best match to your base diameter.
