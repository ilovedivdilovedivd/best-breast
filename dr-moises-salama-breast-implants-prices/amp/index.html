Implants or silicone implants for a real look

The profile and the placement of the implants will achieve the real (natural) look that you are seeking.

To understand your options, consult with 3 experienced and expert board certified plastic surgeons. Choose the surgeon and surgical plan that best meets your goals/objectives.

During your consultation with a board certified plastic surgeon measurements of your breast base width will be taken to determine the size of breast implant that will fit your breast.

Because your nipple appears to be below the level of the fold under your breast you may also need a breast lift.

Am I the right candidate for breast augmentation? I’m 23 years old, 5’6″, 120 lbs, with a breast size of a very small B

I would make sure that your plastic surgeon understands your goals as well as your concerns after surgery. It is important that you and your plastic surgeon have a candid discussion about implant size and type.

By altering the breast with an implant, you will stretch the skin and likely constrict the overlying breast tissue. This is not necessarily a bad thing, but a consequence of the enlargement provided by the implant.

Breast Implants do not really lift the breasts

Implants CAN fill the upper part of the breast if it is hollow, and provide more roundness and fullness. If you feel you nipple is too low, or the fold of skin under the breast is too long (i.e. you can hold a pencil or two under your breast) then you would benefit from a lift. Placing implants in a breast that is already a bit long will make it longer over time.
You will need a full lift or anchor scar at the same time to achieve an elevation of your Breasts. Scars are inevitable.

Breast augmentation and lift question

You would benefit most from a breast augmentation with a lift. Although no one wants the scars, it is sometimes necessary in order to achieve the given goals.

It is true that an implant will fill up a deflated breast, but it really won’t lift them to the degree that a mastopexy will. Just like a hammer is not a good substitute for a screwdriver, you always want to use the right tool for the job.

Having said that, you don’t have to do both together at the same time. Many patients will be happy to have the size / volume of the implant. You can always comeback at a later date to do the lift if you felt it still had too much sag.
You should have several consultations with board certified plastic surgeons before making any final decisions. As always, best to be healthy, no smoking, and make sure any health concerns you have are managed by your primary care md.

Lift or no lift

It is tricky to comment without examining you but you do have some ptosis and asymmetry. It really is dependent on what you wish to achieve and the volume you wish for. It is one of those cases where an augment only may not be enough to place the nipple into the ideal position but it may be a result you are happy with.

If you wanted to maximise faking a lift without mastopexy scars then an overprojected anatomical implant would be the best way of achieving this.

To maximise the upper pole a tall height may be best.
A mastopexy could be performed later if needed if you went down this route. If your priority is upper fullness and you want high profile rounds then in my opinion you will need a one stage mastopexy implant to achieve this.

You need to consult with someone experienced in augmentation and mastopexy implant in weight loss patients. In weight loss often a one stage mastopexy implant is better than trying to fake a lift with an implant but it depends on what you are looking to achieve and how comfortable you are with the scars of a mastopexy.

Generally speaking, breast implants do not cause stretch marks.

Although most patients who undergo breast augmentation do not experience any stretch marks or striae, some can. A person usually develops stretch marks or tears in the dermis during periods of rapid growth in the presence of high circulating hormontes (steroids), for example, during puberty or pregnancy.
If one’s skin envelope is quite tight and desires a large augmentation, this type of patient could potentially suffer from some stretch marks, depending on their genetics. These stretch marks initally are red in nature but whiten with time.

Usually post partum women with stretch marks find that breast implants improve the appearance of their stretch marks.

Double bubble after breast augmentation

Recognizing which patients are more at risk to get a double bubble is the first step in the process. There are maneuvers that can be carried out at the time of surgery to minimize the double crease (bubble) but depending on the patients anatomy, and the goals of the surgery, there may yet be some degree of the old fold remaining.

Second Consultation

A second consultation is an excellent idea. It will help you to clarify your goals in advance of the date of surgery. This approach will assist the surgeon in providing you with the outcome that you desire.

Implant profiles

There can be a 4 to 7% change in breast volume during the first year after a breast augmentation. This is why patients will often request one size larger at surgery. As far as implant profiles it will look much different on every individual.

A thin woman with a narrow base diameter, not much breast tissue with a large HP implant will look very round. Another option to maintain upper pole fullness is using a shaped implant. With a round implant there is a tendency to settle to the bottom of the implant pocket thus losing some upper pole fullness.

Breast lift probably best for you

According to your picture, you would probably benefit most from a breast lift. This is because your breasts are affected greatly by ptosis and implants alone would most likely not achieve a good enough result.

Breast Implant placement

There are a lot of things to consider regarding the placement of the implant. Frame size is not an indication to place the implant over vs. under or the reverse. Frame size guides implant size that will be appropriate.

Personally, I prefer under the muscle (smooth, round, inframammary incision) in order to minimize the problems you mentioned. To minimize the risk of ripples even more, silicone gel implants are the way to go. If you feel uncomfortable, you should seek another opinion.

Generally, it is also best to have the opportunity to try on several sizers to see how the implants fit your frame.

As far as size is concerned, you are not that far off from your goal pics. You do NOT need a lift. I would suggest doing the rice test at home. Take a pair of nylons, fill each with dry rice ( 1 ounce = approx 30 cc ) and wear that inside your bra for a day.

It will give you a good idea of how much added volume will make you feel. Guessing, I would estimate something in the 325-375 cc range would fit your goals. I would suggest a round mod plus type implant.

Use a textured implant if you are an athlete and place them under the muscle. I don’t think you need to have the contoured tear drop type in this case. The general rule with implants:
1. if there is a slight size difference, implants will make the size look more even.
2. if there is a slight shape / nipple position difference, implants will sometimes make this worse or more obvious because the result is magnified.

You should have several consultations with board certified plastic surgeons before making any final decisions. As always, best to be healthy, no smoking, and make sure any health concerns you have are managed by your primary care md.
