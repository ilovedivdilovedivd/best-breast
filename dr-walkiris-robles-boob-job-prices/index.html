Breast implants can last a while without complications

Breast implants generally have a warranty of 10 years, but many patients have found that they can last much longer without any complications. The concern, though, is that the longer you wait, the higher the rupture rate rises.

If an implant ruptures, surgery to remove and/or exchange the implant becomes much more complicated.

Volume v. dimension

In absolute terms, 650cc. is quite large. It is also possibly difficult to fit that size into a pocket comfortably if you are truly an A cup. You also have to be careful about trying to jump to a large size from a small even though that is what augmentation is all about, there are more implant complications as the proportion of breast that is implant increases.

The dimensional approach measures the width of the breast to determine width of the implant that fits comfortably behnd the breast. You also have to pay attention to where the nipple is located relative to your inframammary fold.

If you have a short and wide breast, you may be limited due to this nipple position. Once you determine the comfortable width, you can increase volume by increase profile or projection. The final volume is the result of this analysis, it is not the determinant of it.

Nipple/Areola Pointing Downward after Breast Augmentation Normal?

What you are experiencing is quite commonly experienced after breast augmentation surgery. It may take several additional months for your breast implants to fully “settle” into their pockets. Some patients referred to this process as “drop and fluffing”.

As this occurs, it is very likely that you will notice your nipple/areola complexes pointing more forward, as opposed to downward. Ask your plastic surgeon if he/she would recommend the use of a bandeau or massage to help with this process.

Treatment of tuberous breast.

The difference between a tuberous breast and a constricted breast can sometimes be confusing. A tuberous breast looks like Snoopy’s nose. A constricted breast looks more like a cone shape.

In your case based upon your photographs I am seeing a mild amount of constriction more on the left than on the right. If you choose an implant with an appropriate diameter, the diameter of the implant will be greater the diameter of the fold of your breast which is tight or constricted.
Your plastic surgeon will have to give a little bit more effort to release the lower pole of the breast so that the breast takes on the shape of the implant. If your breast does not take on the shape of the implant you may see a breast, sitting on top of the implant.

This is known as double bubble. The treatment would be a Periareolar lift or tightening. In someone like yourself with mild constriction I generally will place the implant, wait about 3 to 6 months and then consider further tightening only if necessary in the future.

Based upon your photographs I doubt that you would need it.

BREAST IMPLANT POSITION

The most important factors to consider in determiining implant position are the amount of soft tissue (breast tissue) available in the upper pole of the breast, the size of the implant and whether you prefer saline or gel.
It is my preference to place all saline implants under the muscle because of the risk of rippling and visibilty. The amount of soft tissue coverage is determined by measuring the thickness of breast tissue in the upper pole.

Generally speaking you need about an inch with smaller gel implants you may be able to get away with less coverage. The biggest disadvantages to placing implants under the muscle are implant displacement and implant distortion by contraction of the muscle.
