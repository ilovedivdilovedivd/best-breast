Draining fluid in post op visit. Is this a simple procedure?
Fluid collection post op
Aug 27, 2018
Some procedures (tummy tuck in particular) are prone to forming a fluid collection post op. This is called a seroma. Often this is self-limiting and reabsorbed by your body, helped by wearing a compression garment. Sometimes your Plastic Surgeon may choose to drain the seroma. This is a simple procedure that can be performed in the office with a needle and syringe. It’s usually relatively painless, but may need to be performed more than once until the seroma settles down.
