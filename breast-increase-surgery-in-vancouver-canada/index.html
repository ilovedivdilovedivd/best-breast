Breast Augmentation Following Breast Reduction

Vancouver Breast augmentation following breast reduction may seem odd at first but actually there can be real benefits to this. Some patients have large and droopy breasts and want a lift but are not particularly unhappy with their size.

In those cases, a lift may not be very effective but a reduction in the droopy breast tissue and replacement with an implant that can sit higher can be beneficial. This is often done in two stages with the reduction done first and the augmentation months later.

Some patients don’t plan on an augmentation following a reduction but may find that, as a result of having children or weight loss, that they desire more fullness. A note of caution: it is important that your surgeon is aware of how the previous surgery was performed to minimize the risk of poor blood supply that could interfere with healing.

Make sure to consult with a board certified plastic surgeon as you investigate your options. .

Will pregnancy ruin my breast implants

If you become pregnant, your implants in Vancouver will not be affected, but the overlying skin and breast tissue can change. Most of the time, pregnancy hormones will cause the lobular tissue to increase in size to get ready to breastfeed.

This will cause an increase in the size of your breasts as well as stretch the overlying skin. Problems may arise once you deliver or discontinue with breast feeding. The breast tissue will shrink, however the overlying skin may stay stretched out.
If this is the case, the skin may sag over the implant giving you a “snoopy” deformity (implant sits high and skin hangs over the lower part of implant). In this case, you will require a breast lift.

Reducing Pain and Recovery Time

Just as every patient is different ( pain tolerance, recovery time etc. etc.), alas, every Plastic Surgeon is different as well. So you will get a lot of different answers to this question.

First and foremost, make sure that you have this discussion with each surgeon that you plan on visiting for a consultation.

For my practice, I believe the single most important thing we do to decrease pain and recovery times is to have patients be as active as possible for the first few days after surgery ( but no exercise).
So walking, moving their arms, going out to dinner etc., is encouraged. I find that the patients that do not follow our advice are the ones that take longer to return to their normal daily activities.

It needs to match your body

The high-profile designs will not have as “natural” of a slope as moderate profile, but choosing moderate would often mean a smaller size in order to fit in the base diameter.
