Saline implants above the muscle

The choice between placing and implant above or below the muscle mainly involves how much breast tissue you have to provide adequate coverage for the implant. If you have enough tissue coverage, then you don’t see the folds and ripples of the implant.

Saline ripples more than silicone gel filled implants but coverage over the implant is the key. The other important factor is the size of implant. If you choose too large of an implant, no matter where it is placed, you will see the implant edges and the folds.

Review your options with your surgeon and choose a size of implant that matches your body and a location and will give you the best outcome.

No timeframe for removal of ruptured saline implant

A ruptured saline implant will not cause injury or illness and does not need to be removed with haste. It would typically be quite safe for you to have the ruptured implant in your body for some time until it is convenient to have it removed and replaced.
Do breast implants need to be changed every 10 years?

This is a very common question and misconception. Implants do not have an expiration date when they need to be replaced. In truth, you do not need to have implants replaced unless you are having a specific problem with the implants.

This could be a number of things from a rupture, scar tissue making the implant feel firm, scar tissue shifting the implant, sagging of the breast tissue, etc. If you are happy with your implants and are not having any problems with them, then you don’t need to replace them.

Natural looking breast implants

In general silicone implants offer a more natural feel. They mimic natural breast tissue better than saline. Getting results that look and feel natural depends on several factors such as implant placement, size, and type.
If implants are too large for your body the risk of rippling increases so you should consider this as well.

Implant profile

Each implant manufacturer has different profiles of implants that they offer. The two main implant manufacturers (Allergen and Mentor) each have their own unique terms and different profiles – why make things easy… But the important concept is that if you were to consider an implant with a volume, of say, 300 cc, for the purposes of this example, and were to look at it in a low, medium, and high profile, the dimensions of each of the implants would be different.

The low profile 300 cc implant would be wider with a larger footprint but project less as compared to the high profile 300 cc implant which would project more but have a much smaller footprint. I generally use the medium (moderate plus) profile implants for most patients but will consider a higher profile implant if I am trying to fit a larger implant in a narrow chested woman.

Be active in choosing your implant size

Choosing the proper implant size involves close communication between patient and surgeon. In my office we carefully discuss each patient’s size goals. Then each patient works closely with my nurse and me using implant sizers to determine which size will meet these goals.

My patients feel empowered by participating in making this important decision, and we have an excellent rate of patient satisfaction using this method.

Breast Massages After Surgery

Disclaimer: Every surgeon has different post-operative instructions, or preferences, regarding post-operative care. You should listen to your own surgeon. In my practice, this is how I instruct patients: Starting on the 7th day after surgery, you will also want to perform breast massages as directed, two times a day.
Breast massaging may prevent scar formation, assists the skin in accommodating the implant and helps you get used to the feel of your new breasts.

Don’t sacrifice beauty for breast size

Proportionality is the key. The patient is the one who ultimately will choose the implant size. However, be careful choosing a cup size that is way out of proportion to your body. A wise Plastic Surgeon can advise you on what would look propotionally best.

In my practice we then”negotiate” either larger or smaller based on patient request, rib width, how tight the muscle and skin is, etc. Your body may not “fit” a very large implant– it would continue around your side and look unnatural – not a pretty breast.

You should ask your doctor what is the largest size that will fit your frame and still look somewhat natural.

Breast sizing

Sizing is a very personal thing and should definitely be discussed with your surgeon to help you come to your decision. Using the rice bag method or sizers in clinic is very easy to do and will give you a good idea.
However, everyone’s breasts are different in terms of pre-existing size, elasticity of the skin, breast width, etc and careful discussion is therefore required. Profile is very confusing to patients but essentially is determined by (and is a consequence of) your breast width and desired size.

Breast lift effect with breast augmentation.

There is a degree of lifting effect with breast implant augmentation as the implants “fill” the breast soft tissue “sac”. I would say in my practice that most women with mild to moderate degrees of saggy breasts and low set nipples will be satisfied with the lifting effect of breast augmentation.

Those with moderate to severe saggy breast and nipples that are at the lower breast will not be satisfied. For those boarderline cases, I typically recommend that the patient first do a breast augmentation first and see how they like the result.
