Saline vs Silicone Implants

There is a price difference between saline and silicone implants. The silicone implants are more expensive. The exact dollar difference depends on the type of silicone implant your surgeon would be utilizing.

Breast feeding after breast implants or breast augmentation

Breast feeding can be affected by the presence of breast implants but not usually. A breast augmentation does not typically disturb the breast gland nor the ducts and therefore is unlikely to affect breast feeding.

It is probably less likely to affect breast feeding if the implants are placed from an incision in the arm pit or under the breasts and more likely if it is through the nipple. Studies has shown that even silicone breast implants are safe for breast feeding children as enough silicone does not enter the blood stream or milk to cause any problems.

Breast Feeding with Breast Implants

The data that I’ve seen suggests that five percent of women without surgery are unable to breast feed and after implants the statistics do not change. A nipple incision does cut a few of your dozen or so breast ducts and may make initiating breast feeding more difficult but in my experience this is not a big issue.

An alternative approach to consider would be a subpectoral (partial) transaxillary approach to avoid the nipple incision. I would remind you that breasts do change after pregnancy and breast feeding whether implants are present or not.

Once in Hundreds of Patients

In over 20 years of practice, and 100’s of breast augmentations, I have only been aware of one patient who developed stretch marks. Of course, this is not a statistically valid study, but I thought you would want to know something specific.
I agree with other comments that very large implants would be more likely to cause this. I think your skin and deeper tissues are the main determinates. I doubt that you can use a product that will prevent stretch marks.

If you had prior rapid weight gain or prior pregnancies without developing any stretch marks, that is favorable for you.

Weight gain from breast implants

Wecome to middle age! The weight that middle aged ladies gain is almost always on the torso. Your story is not at all unusual. You may want to consider having your implants removed and having a breast lift.

This is an operation I perform on middle aged ladies fairly often and they are usually very pleased to be less top heavy, nice and perky and implant free.

Shopping for New Bras after a BA surgery

You want to wait the full 4-6 weeks after surgery before moving over to a regular bra.

During the 4-6 time period is when your breast will be settling into place and you want to make sure they are in perfect position when they are in the healing process.

If you did not have a lift with your augmentation you must wait at least 6 weeks after surgery to wear an underwire bra.

A combination of factors determines breast implant placement.

You can best explore your options and discuss your appearance goals in a consultation with a board-certified plastic surgeon. An in-person meeting can help your plastic surgeon to evaluate your breast tissue and anatomy to determine optimal breast implant placement.

Select plastic surgeons offer imaging technology, such as Vectra 3D imaging, to help you visualize what your final results will look like. Given your dimensions, I’d lean towards submuscular implants (under the muscle).

Your breast tissue will provide coverage for the implants for a more natural result, and once you’re fully healed you should be able to resume weightlifting with no restrictions.

Breast augmentation and activity

My patients are up and about the day of surgery. In fact, I recommend going out for dinner or movie. They shower next day when the wrapping bra comes off in my office.

Breast lift or implants or both

A breast lift is needed when the tissues sag and the nipple is low. An implant may fill the envelope but will not lift the breast.
Breast Implant Encapsulation

If one has implants, certainly one can have encapsulation. As they age, you may have hardness, pain, or asymmetry. Usually this can be noticed early with saline implants or later with silicone gel implants. The cause is unknown.

It is much less infrequent with saline implants. If it does not improve symptomatically over a period of 6 months then one may require a capsulectomy or capsulotomy to correct the capsular contracture.

Breast Implants and Breast Lift

Unfortunately, Implants alone would not yield a good result in your particular situation. Having said that, you may be a candidate for minimal incision breast lift procedure with implants.

You would need to be examined in person to see if you’re a candidate for this type of a lift/silicone anatomic textured implant option.

What’s the best way to avoid rippling?

The best way to avoid rippling is to first avoid saline filled implants. Secondly, place the silicone filled implants behind the muscle. this gives more coverage of the implants. Thirdly, I would also recommend a newer implant that has a higher fill such as the Inspira gel implants made by Natrelle.

Breast implants and airplanes

On a normal plane flight after a new augmentation, you may feel some pressure changes, just like in your ears. if there is a sigificant pressure change in the airplane cabin, you have bigger issues than your implants going on!

Implant size

hope this help with your decision. Yes it is true the same implant can look different person to person. It all depends on body type and how much breast tissue someone has before surgery. For example, someone that wears a 32 band width and an A cup will need less volume to be larger than someone that wears a 36 band width and is a B cup.
I advise you to see a Board Certified Plastic Surgeon for your surgery.

Pregnancy can affect the breasts but not so much the breast implants

When the breasts expand as they fill with breast milk and then deflate, they can sag. How much they will sag is largely genetic (your mom is a good reference). Implants are unlikely to affect this problem very much.

Certainly I have seen many women with sagging breasts after pregnancies that never had implants! A benefit of having implants at the time of pregnancy is that they will not shrivel to nothing after the pregnancy.

So I don’t think you need to worry about the implants. If you are going to need a breast lift later, you will likely need one regardless of whether you have implants. I attached a link to my website in case you wish to read more.
