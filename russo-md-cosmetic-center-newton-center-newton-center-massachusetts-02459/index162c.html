Joseph A. Russo, MD

Name: Joseph A. Russo, MD
Statement: Dr. Russo is a Board-certified, Harvard-trained Plastic Surgeon in the Boston area. His private practice in Boston, MA has been running since 1991 and has evolved into a full-service surgical and cosmetic center. He is a member of the American Society of Plastic Surgeons (ASPS). With a passion for both surgical and nonsurgical cosmetic treatment options, he is considered a national leader in the medical aesthetics industry and reigns as a Top 5 Allergan® account and Top 20 Merz® account. When he’s not seeing patients, Dr. Russo dedicates his time educating and supporting other medical professionals in the art, science, practice, and business of medical aesthetics. He opened his school, formerly known as Aesthetic Institute of Massachusetts - now Aesthetic Mentor, in 2013, where he and his educating staff teach doctors, NP’s, and nurses injectables such as Botox Cosmetic, and Juvederm® dermal fillers. The school has locations in Waltham, MA and Glastonbury, CT. Dr. Russo also supervises Medical Aesthetics Associates (MAA), the most elite, compliant practice of nurse providers in Massachusetts, and is building his national brand Aesthetic Mentor™ to share his knowledge and support others in the medical aesthetics industry. Dr. Russo and his staff also offer cutting edge cosmetic procedures and skincare products to rejuvenate the face. Dr. Russo has extensive experience in the latest laser technology, and he is a leader in the field of laser hair removal in Boston. Dr. Russo believes it is important to know his patients as individuals, so he can understand their desires, personalize their care, and customize their results. Dr. Russo’s loyal client base continues to expand by word-of-mouth, thanks to his reputation as a top Plastic Surgeon in Boston, MA.

Do facelifts and neck lifts turn out worse when the patient has had RF microneedling previously?
facelift
Feb 20, 2020
Anything that causes injury and subsequent inflammation in the skin can cause scar tissue to form. This definitely makes the surgery more difficult, but surgeons are used to working under these circumstances, and it should not affect your result

Feb 20, 2020
In general, once a woman loses fat from her face, it is difficult to gain it back despite weight gain. It is not uncommon to have filler placed to regain that youthful look

Is it safe to have Botox in the neck with health conditions?
botox
Jan 16, 2020
It is safe to have Botox even with a thyroid condition. There is no interaction with the thyroid gland as the botox is injected into the muscle which is superficial to the thyroid
