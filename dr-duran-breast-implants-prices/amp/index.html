Sizing implants

Implant sizing is limited by the width of your chest. Choose an implant that is too wide and the edges will be very visible and possible touching inthe middle, which is not natural.

For your frame, 500cc is a very large implant, although that is just an opinion. The best way to size implants is by trying them out in yout surgeon’s office. 3d modeling computer programs can also be helpful.

Since you don’t have a large amount of breat tissue to begin with, you run the risk of having very visible implants that you can easily feel through tje skin if you go too large.

The pictures of your ideal results have women with much larger native breasts and may be inpossible to achieve and maintain a natural look with your frame.

Saline implants

You are a good candidate for breast augmentation. Both saline and silicone are safe implants. It is upto you and your physician about type and size of the implants.

Breast implants after breast feeding

Some women’s breasts change dramatically after breast feeding – some never change size. Wait until you are sure your breasts are no longer changing size – Some women get smaller, some larger breasts after breast feeding.

Once you notice breast size is stable, wait 6 weeks before surgery, to be sure no further changes.

Implants Only

Yes, you can get away with just implants. Just remember the bigger you go, the more they weigh and the further south they will head. Implants don’t defy gravity so I encourage you to wear good support as much as you can.

Asymmetric Breast

No one’s left and right side of the body is identical, the same is true with breast. However, if the degree of asymmetry (difference) in breasts size, shape, nipple position, areolar size and sagginess is significant, every attempt should be made to make the two sides as similar as possible when the breast augmentation is performed.

Dr Agustina Duran

The top view photograph shows a significant degree of asymmetry than the frontal views. Not only there is size difference between the two breasts, there seems to be a different degree of ptosis (sagginess) between the two breasts.

During the consultations a set of different measurements must be made to determine the differences in sizes and ptosis (sagginess). The right breast appear a little more ptotic and the right nipple is lower than the left side.

During the breast augmentation, different sample (temporary sizers) will have to be used to determine the exact implant size for each side. In regards to the breast ptosis (sagginess) a mastopexy (breast lift) should be designed to elevate the breast tissue off the chest wall.

In addition, the breast lift should place the both nipples at about the same level and if desired by the patient the areola should be made smaller.
Are your implants silicone gel?

Do you know if your implants are silicone gel filled? Gel filled implants were not available in the U. S. for first time breast augmentation patients ten years ago. They were re-approved for this use by the FDA in 2006 after 14 years of research confirming that essentially there is no such thing as silicone poisoning and no cause and effect between silicone gel implants and disease.

Pursue the issue further with your doctor, who may refer you on to a rheumatologist for further work up.


Natural appearance and implant placement

An implant can appear very natural placed either under or over the muscle. It can be quite difficult to determine based on appearance alone. Often a patient would need to contract their pectoralis muscle for a surgeon to tell whether the implant is submuscular or over the muscle (subglandular).
With larger implant sizes, a submuscular position can look more natural. In the position, the upper half of the implant can be slightly compressed by the pectoralis muscle giving a teardrop shape.

In an implant over the muscle, more fullness of the top of half the implant may be more evident.

Another consideration is rippling. If a patient’s skin is thin, rippling of the implant shell may show through the skin. With submuscular placement, the extra layer of muscle may prevent this. Conversely, some of my patients have developed chest muscles or are competitive bodybuilders.

In these patients, a contraction of their chest muscle may cause a more visible shift of the implant which is not usually an issue in most patients. In these patients, a more “natural” appearance may be with an implant over the muscle.
Natural does mean different things to different people. To give a patient the most natural result, their anatomy, activities and desired size may all play a role in choosing the best surgery to fit that specific patient.

Small Breast Implants

The size of the breast implant is directly related to the base width of the breast.

This is the distance measured from the lateral breast boarder at the anterior axillary fold to the medial breast edge at the sternum.

Generally speaking any implant that is less then about 2cm less then the breast base width will not support the breast appropriately and will cause gross deformity. Most breast base widths for augmentation run in the 11-15cm area.

Therefore, the base width of say a 100cc implant would be much too small to appropriately support the breast. Realistically speaking a small implant like this would be lost under the muscle with little to no appreciable enhancement.
The purpose of an enhancement is to create proportion with the shoulders, hips and buttock. So surgery should be performed to achieve an aesthetically pleasing result.
