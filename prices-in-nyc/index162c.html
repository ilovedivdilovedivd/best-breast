Breast Implant Procedure Prices In New York City

Breast implants in New York City themselves, either saline or silicone, pose no health risks to your body-even if they rupture.

There has been significant research and studies performed on this topic over the last several decades and the FDA has approved both saline and silicone gel implants for cosmetic breast augmentation.

The implants do not last a lifetime and you will likely require another surgery in the future. The implants can become infected, which is extremely rare.

The implants can rupture, which is in the 1-10% range over ten years depending on silicone vs saline.

The implants can develop capsular contracture, which is a hard type of scar tissue which forms around the implant, sometimes causing painful symptoms requiring their replacement.

You should consult with a board certified plastic surgeon for a more comprehensive review of all the risks pertaining to breast implants and the surgery itself.

Breast implants in NYC over or under the muscle

The good news is that your situation and question is not unique. Many t hin, athletic women seek breast augmentation. The answer depends on what risks you are willing to tolerate. On top of the muscle, the implant will not move appreciably with muscle contraction.

I see that as the only advantage. Under the muscle, there is a significantly lower risk of encapsulation, better mammograms and less visibility of the implant (what many call the fake look). You are less likely to see rippling of the implant under the muscle.

Despite our best efforts, the implant will move with contraction of the muscle in some patients. Fortunately, it doesn’t happen often. My bias is obviously for under the muscle with a dual plane approach.

It has worked well for many femal athletes.

Breast asymmetry is very common, especially if your breasts were not symmetric before your procedure. I suggest waiting until you are fully healed before addressing your concerns with your surgeon.

Stretch Marks Uncommon from Breast Implants

The most likely women to get stretch marks after breast implants are young (usually under 21), and have very firm tight skin of the breasts. if the implant is very large and the skin is very tight, rapid expansion with placement of an implant will be more likely to result in stretch marks.

placeing the implant under the muscle can be protective and reduce the stress on the skin to some degree. if you think about stretch marks as “cracks” in the deep and firm layer of the skin (the dermis) that leaves the thin epidermis thinned out trying to keep the cracks “covered”, it makes more sense.

pregnancy, rapid weight gain, and puberty (with high hormone levels and rapid development of hips and breasts) are much more likely to cause stretch marks than implants. your plastic surgeon will be able to give you an idea as to whether your skin condition and the phase of life you are in make your risk of stretch marks higher than average.

stretch marks are, however, generally not seen often as a result of placing an implant in the breast for breast augmentation. a small benefit of breast implants for women who already have stretch marks, is that stretch marks generally look much better when over-stretched and deflated breast skin is filled up with an implant!

Recommended Waiting Period between Breast Feeding and Breast Augmentation

The consideration has to do with the fact that the breast milk ducts are not totally sterile and that placing implants through this environment may be associated with a slightly higher rate of capsular contracture and implant infection.

The generally recommended waiting period between end of breast feeding and a breast augmentation is between 3 and 6 months. At 9 months, you should be able to go ahead and have the procedure.

Breast Augmentation Above or Below the Muscle

There are many advantages to having a breast augmentation below the muscle.

1. It looks more natural especially on thin patients or those with little breast tissue as the muscle is one more layer hiding the implant.

2. When you get mammograms, the detection of cancer is the same as without implants when they take an extra view as the implant is totally below all the breast tissue.

3. It decreases the risk of capsular contracture.

The disadvantages are that you may move the implants slightly when you use your pectoralis muscles, and you cannot do pectoralis work-outs for 3 months after augmentation to allow the implant to settle down into the pocket.

The amount of time an implant will last will vary from person to person. Just because they are 10 years old or more does not mean they need to be removed unless they are ruptured .

While there is a role for the transaxillary approach in breast augmentation, every perception you have about the benefits of the inframammary or the sub inframmary approach is correct. It is far from old school and is still the workhorse of incisions for breast augmentation in New York City.

Options for breast implant placement

By options for placement there are several considerations-the two that most people think about are location of the incision and then position of the implant in relation to the muscle. Incision location is usually decided based on surgeon and patient preference.

In a patient with a well defined breast crease (infra-mammary fold) I personally prefer to place the incision there because it is well-hidden. However, others prefer different approaches. As far as implant location, if you have very thin tissues I almost always place the implant under the muscle; it gives you extra coverage over the upper inner half of the implant where, in thin patients, the edge of the implant is likely to show.

As far as on top of the muscle versus below the muscle thare are several pros and cons. On top of the muscle-less painful post-op (short term issue and I advise patients to not consider this), less breast tissue visualized on mammography (has not translated into a medical problem for patients in terms of delayed diagnosis of breast cancer, etc.), possibly higher incidence of capsular contracture although this is very murky, no risk of distortion with activity like the implant jumping when flex pecs.

Below muscle-more painful, more tissue visualized on mammo, may have lower capsular contracture rates, can have distortion of implant with pec muscle activity, implants may drift down and out over time.

Fuurther considerations-are you a body-builder? They prefer on top of the muscle. Another-do you need a breast lift at same time? If you do I prefer on top of the muscle as long as your tissues are not too thin.

Large Breast Implants after Surgery

Patients are often unaware that swelling plays a major role in the postoperative course after breast augmentation. It is important to maintain a good rapport with your surgeon and allow the swelling to subside before embarking on any plan for definitive size reduction.

Breast Implants in NYC – Incisions

Nobody wants visible incisions and they are, in general, pretty well hidden. But dependin on the type of implants you have, the location of the incisions and specifics of your skin type, it is not realistic to expect them to be completely hidden.

The most common incisions are periareolar (more specifically and accurately infra-areolar, since they are around the lower portion of the areola), infra-mammary (under the breast), axillary (armpit) and umbilical (belly-button). The last is available for saline implants only and the first two are the most commonly used.

The best thing is for you to try to decide which type of implants you want and then go visit a few different plastic surgeons to get some ideas of the options for you.
