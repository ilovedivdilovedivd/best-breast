Minnesota Breast Augmentation Prices

Minnesota implant costs are based on type (silicone or saline) and on shape (round or anatomic). They are not based on volume (size in cc’s).

The cost for the procedure is a combination of the implant cost, surgeon’s operating fee, anesthesia services, and the facility fee for the operating room.

Silicone implants do cost more money per implant than saline implants..

Choosing to go larger

The choice to go larger is not much different at three months when the tissue are likely fully recovered and the implant accommodated compared to three years later. This is not a “revision”. It’s a choice that you want to go larger regardless of what you thought when you had it done.

One of the reasons to use an inframammary crease incision for the original procedure is the possibility that you may need or choose to replace implants in the future. With an existing inframammary crease incisional scar, the option to replace your existing implants with larger ones is fairly straightforward if you want to maintain your natural and non-distorted looking result.

The replacement implant should be the same diameter or at most only slightly wider than your existing implants. It’s the profile (forward volume) that can be increased and your tissues will accommodate it.

If you have the lowest forward profile round implant you can increase it to the medium forward profile or even the highest profile.

If you have a medium profile implant now (not called that in gel-filled implants) you can only increase to a high profile implant of about the same width and increase your apparent cup size by about 1/2 cup.

If you are already at the highest profile implant then you don’t have much option to exchange your existing ones for larger ones that will fit and look right. This is essentially the same as choices for the first augmentation.

Don’t start with a number of cc’s and work backward. You start with an implant that fits the interior width of your breast and then choose a forward profile based on about 1-2 cup sizes of increase and only then look up the number of cc’s.

Another tip is to maintain the details and dimensions of your existing implants. If you decide to replace them years later you will need to know what you have in order to make a good decision on sizing for replacing them.

Breast implant question

It is way too early to make a judgment on how your implants will settle. When implants in Minnesota are placed under the muscle, particularly if you are younger, the implant will initially feel like it’s sitting at the top of your chest.

Your muscle is tight and muscle and skin has not looseed to accommodate for your implant at this point. It will eventually. This may take 3 to 6 months. This is particularly true if you were using a higher profile breast implant that is placed under the muscle.

I agree with massage as this will help the implant settle, and perhaps prevent capsular contracture. Time and patience are important. Express your concerns to your plastic surgeon who can give you advice.

Massage is beneficial for a variety of reasons.

I counsel women with breast implants that massage is useful for the following reasons:

a) it is a biofeedback technique, in which the pain resulting from stretch on the muscle and breast flap is over ridden by the sensation of pressure from the massage;
b) it allows displacement of the implant into the pocket;
c) it reacquaints the woman with her breast and enables her to become familiar with breast self-exam;
d) it may reduce the occurence of capsular contracture;
e) it is a prelude to a woman’s resumption of her personal life.

In general, I instruct breast implant patients to start a week after surgery. For those women who have both a breast implant and lift, we start at two weeks.

I generally don’t recommend massage with textured implants.

Capsular Contracture

Capsular contracture is the biggest problem with breast implants occurring in 15% of women in the FDA study for gel implants. In 85% of women the scar tissue around the implants remain soft, in 15% it gets hard, round and can distort the shape of the breast and even be painful.

Some insurance companies have a covered benefit for treatment of symptomatic capsular contracture’s and you are urged to check with your plastic surgeon. The newest generation textured form stable cohesive gel gummy bear implants (Sientra) have an initial capsular contracture rate of only 2%.

Risks of Breast Augmentation Surgery in Minnesota

Every surgical procedure has risks. Even though breast augmentation is a cosmetic procedure, it’s still surgery, and you should carefully evaluate the risks before you proceed with surgery.


Early postoperative risks include:
  1. infection (national rate 2%, my rate 0.5%)
  2. bleeding (national rate 2%, my rate 0.2%)
  3. nipple sensory changes (about 10 to 15%)

Late postoperative risks include:

Deflation (saline) or rupture (silicone gel). Implant failure is the greatest single risk of surgery, since the implants will probably not last as long as you do.

Capsular contracture (10 – 40%, depending on type of implant and location of placement). This refers to tightening of scar tissue surrounding the implant, causing the implant to look or feel hard.
Unsatisfactory cosmetic results. The implants could be too large, too small, too high, too low, too far apart, too close together.

This list of risks is by no means all-inclusive, but these are the items I worry about most often. Fortunately, the vast majority of women undergoing breast augmentation have an uncomplicated postoperative course. 95% say they would do it again.

High Profile Breast Implants in Minnesota

A natural looking breast is typically thought to be a breast that is full with some, but not excessive, fullness in the upper portion of the breast. Achieving this appearance after breast augmentation depends on many factors.

It depends on the condition of the breast before breast augmentation, whether or not the implant is placed under or above the muscle, and the construction of the pocket in which the implant is placed.

More important than the selection of what type of implant to use, is selecting the right plastic surgeon. An experienced plastic surgeon can make an accurate assessment of all of these factors and help choose the breast implant for you.

A high-profile implant has the same actual volume as a moderate-plus or moderate-profile implant. The dimensions of the implant differ. A high-profile implant is more round and has a more narrow base diameter. Consequently, there is more projection of the implant which is the measurement between the chest wall and the most projecting point of your breast.

In properly selected patients, high-profile implants will give a natural looking breast.

Breast augmentation revision and pain

Pain after breast implant revision in Minnesota depends upon many factors including: the degree of capsule surgery that you need, placement of the implants, and incision location. If the reaugmentation is simply an exchange of implants (either larger or smaller) in the same place then the discomfort associated with surgery is typically less than the original operation.

When changing the position of the implants, performing extensive surgery on the implant capsule, or even changing the incision the discomfort may be as much or more than the original augmentation. In general, most revision breast augmentation patients report less discomfort than from their previous augmentation.

Does breast augmentation give a breast lift?

It is a great and very common question that you are asking. Breast implants can give some lift to the breasts after augmentation. The larger volume implants that are placed the more lift that you achieve.

If you are only interested in a small increase of volume with the implants then a breast lift will be needed at the same time to achieve optimal results. Yes there will be more scars with a breast lift, but the enhanced results make it worthwhile.
