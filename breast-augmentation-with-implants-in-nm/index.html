Experience will vary

The average lifespan of implants manufactured in the US is 10-15 years, and the most common reasons they would need to be replaced are because they rupture, or because of capsular contracture (firmness and shrinkage of the connective tissue surrounding the implant) which may or may not cause discomfort and/or distortion of the breast.

If you are having problems- whether that be dissatisfaction with the changes in appearance of the breasts, pain, or loss of volume, I would consult your Board Certified Plastic Surgeon for an opinion.

You may not necessarily need to consider surgery for new implants, but it is best to keep in mind that, while our modern implants are much better than those of the past, they remain man-made devices that should be expected to eventually fail and need to be replaced.

In most cases, this replacement of the implants and an aesthetically pleasing result is most simple and likely if the problem is addressed early in the process (especially if your implants are silicone filled). (Armando Soto, MD, FACS, Orlando Plastic Surgeon)

Breast Implant Size

Most women are looking for a natural augmentation in New Mexico and to achieve that natural look it is very important that the width of the implants match the width of your breasts.

Another consideration is the style of implant. Implants come in 3 different styles moderate profile, moderate plus and high profile.

The difference between them is how much they project off of the chest wall. The moderate profile are the most natural and the high profile give a much more augmented look.

The last thing to consider is if the implant is overfilled (referring to saline implants), overfilled saline implants also tend to produce an augmented look. (Bernard A. Shuster, MD, FACS, Miami Plastic Surgeon)

Breast Implant Size Selection

One patient’s too small is another patient’s too big. Choosing the size of breast implants is a completely personal decision.

I would advise that you bring your plastic surgeon pictures of breasts that you believe are too big, too small, and just right. This will ensure that your plastic surgeon will be able to understand exactly the result that you desire and your expectations will be the same.

It is important for you to keep in mind that the width of the breast mound will help determine how large of a breast implant can be used in your case while providing you with a natural-looking result. It is impossible to recommend a certain implant size without directly examining a patient; however, if you can convey your expectations to your plastic surgeon he/she will assist you in selecting the most appropriate implant size for you. (Jeffrey Weinzweig, MD, Chicago Plastic Surgeon)

Best Breast Implant Size for You

Choosing the best size, shape, and style of breast implant requires a detailed discussion between the patient and their Plastic Surgeon, followed by a thorough breast examination. A patient’s desired outcome is a major part in choosing the correct implant. Often bringing in a picture of what you would like your result to be is helpful to your Plastic Surgeon. There is a statistic, although not scientific, that when women who had breast implants were asked if they could undergo their breast augmentation again what would they do differently? 90% replied they would have gotten larger implants. Again, I can’t speak for the science of that statement, but it drives home the importance of the preoperative discussion between a patient and their Plastic Surgeon. There are saline implants and silicone implants. A particular size (volume) saline implant is going to look different than the same size silicone implant. There are several different “profiles” of implant, which will also lend to variations in overall appearance despite being the same volume. (William A. Determining the correct breast implant size

Questions about how to determine the right size breast implant are extremely common. There are a number of frequently used methods which can be helpful in determining the correct size for a given patient. Patients should look at before and after photos of patients with a similar body type as them.

This can give them a more realistic idea of what they might look like after the placement of a certain sized implant. Patients can often try on different sized implants in the office. This is never exact, but seeing oneself in the mirror with differing sizes can give patients a ballpark idea. For patients who are not comfortable going to a plastic surgeon just yet, Natrelle offers a pre-consultation kit that contains different sized sizers that can be used in the privacy of your home.

The kit can be ordered on-line, and some plastic surgeons are providing them to patients in the office after an initial consultation. (Kelly Gallego, MD, FACS, Yuba City Plastic Surgeon)
