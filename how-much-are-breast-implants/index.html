How Much Are Breast Implants?

The cost of breast implants varies widely depending on your market, style of implant, implant fill, if you are a straight forward breast augmentation or if you need any additional work for asymmetry, lifting, etc.

In our area, for example, a standard saline breast augmentation ranges in price from $4500 – $5500, and for silicone you can expect about $1000 more. If you opt for the “gummy bear” implants, this can add another $500 to $1000. (Suzanne M. Quardt, MD, Palm Springs Plastic Surgeon)

Cost of Breast Implants Varies

The cost of breast implants varies, depending on surgeon expertise, where the surgeon is located, and the type of implant used (saline versus silicone, round versus anatomic).

The total cost typically includes the surgeon’s fees, the anesthesia fees, and the surgery center fees. In the Dallas area, the total fees may range from $5000-$7500.

Although cost is a factor in every patient’s decision, I would caution anyone from making a decision based solely on cost.

Make sure your doctor is a board certified plastic surgeon who understands your needs and concerns. (Farah Naz Khan, MD, Dallas Plastic Surgeon)

Cost Considerations

Fees vary in different regions of the country. The larger city areas tend to be more costly because the expenses to the surgeon and hospital or ambulatory surgical centers are higher. Make sure that you include the implant fee, the surgeon fee, the anesthesia fee, and the hospital/surgicenter fee when you compare physicians and their fees.

A good rule of thumb for purchasing almost anything, and it is true with saline breast implants, is don’t go to the cheapest and don’t go to the most expensive. You will find that the reasonable fees will not differ much from each other. Then just choose the physician with whom you feel most comfortable.

Also make sure you go to a board-certified plastic surgeon. I can guarantee you that those surgeons who are not board-certified plastic surgeons will be cheaper, and remember those old words of wisdom: “You get what you pay for!” (Louis C. Cutolo, Jr., MD, FACS, New York Plastic Surgeon)
